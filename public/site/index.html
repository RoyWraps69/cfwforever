How long do commercial vehicle wraps last?

Commercial vehicle wraps using premium cast vinyl (Avery Dennison MPI 1105 or 3M IJ180-CV3) are rated for 5–7 years of outdoor use. With proper care — hand washing, avoiding automated brush car washes, and keeping pressure washers below 1,200 PSI — fleet wraps in Chicago regularly last 6–7 years looking sharp despite harsh winters, road salt, and UV exposure.

Cast vinyl is the industry standard for commercial fleet wraps. Cast vinyl conforms to curves and rivets without lifting, unlike cheaper calendered vinyl which shrinks and fails within 1–2 years. Transparent Pricing & Exceptional ROI for Your Chicago Fleet

Investing in a fleet wrap is a strategic marketing decision, and at Chicago Fleet Wraps, we believe in transparent pricing tailored to your specific needs. While exact costs vary based on vehicle size, design complexity, and coverage (partial vs. full wrap), you can generally expect a full wrap for a standard car or small van to range from $3,000 to $5,500. Larger vehicles like Sprinter vans or box trucks typically fall between $4,500 and $7,500 for a comprehensive full wrap.

Consider the return on investment (ROI) that a fleet wrap delivers compared to traditional advertising. A single vehicle wrap can generate 30,000 to 70,000 impressions per day in a busy city like Chicago. Over its 5-7 year lifespan, this equates to millions of brand exposures for a one-time investment. Unlike billboards or print ads with recurring costs, a vehicle wrap works tirelessly for your business 24/7, reaching potential customers from Evanston to Oak Lawn.

Partial wraps and custom lettering offer more budget-friendly options, starting from $800 to $2,500, providing significant brand visibility at a lower entry point. These options are ideal for businesses looking to make a strong impression without the full commitment of a complete vehicle transformation.
