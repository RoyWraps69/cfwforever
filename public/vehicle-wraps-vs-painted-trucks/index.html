Resale Value

This is the category most fleet managers overlook. A wrapped van sells at factory paint resale values because the vinyl is removed before sale, revealing undamaged factory paint protected for the wrap's full lifespan. A custom-painted commercial van sells at a discount — buyers adjust for repaint cost if they want different branding, and non-standard paint colors reduce the buyer pool. For a 5-year-old cargo van with 150,000 miles, the difference in resale value between factory paint (under wrap) and custom paint can be $2,000–$4,000 per vehicle.

WHEN PAINT MAKES SENSE

Paint is the right choice in three specific commercial scenarios: collector or show vehicles where resale at premium value is the goal and wrapping isn't appropriate; vehicles where the owner wants permanent single-color branding with no future rebrand intention and has a 15+ year vehicle retention plan; and vehicles operating in environments where vinyl adhesive fails (certain chemical exposure, extreme heat above 180°F continuous, or underwater marine applications where submerged wrap life is limited).

For standard commercial fleet vehicles — cargo vans, box trucks, Sprinters, pickup trucks — operating in normal business conditions in the Chicago metro area, wraps are the correct choice in every cost and ROI scenario.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
Is it cheaper to wrap or paint a commercial truck?
A commercial vehicle wrap costs $3,750–$10,900 depending on vehicle size. A comparable custom paint job with branded graphics runs $6,000–$25,000. Over a 10-year period with one rebrand cycle, wraps save a 10-van fleet $62,500–$152,500 compared to paint. Wraps also qualify for full Section 179 deduction in year 1; paint is depreciated.
Do vehicle wraps protect or damage paint?
Premium cast vinyl wraps protect factory paint from UV degradation, minor abrasion, and road debris for the life of the wrap. Chicago Fleet Wraps has zero verified paint damage claims in 10+ years. The key is professional installation on clean, undamaged paint and professional removal — never DIY removal using heat guns above 160°F.
How long does a vehicle wrap last versus paint?
Avery Dennison MPI 1105 and 3M IJ180-CV3 cast vinyl wraps last 5–7 years on commercial vehicles in Chicago's climate. The branded graphics on a custom paint job (typically vinyl decals applied over paint) last 3–5 years before UV fading. A fully painted branded finish using automotive-grade UV paint lasts longer but costs $15,000+ per van — making the per-year cost equivalent to or higher than wraps.

Making the Right Marketing Investment Decision

Choosing the right marketing channel for your business requires understanding not just the upfront costs, but the total cost of ownership over time. Vehicle wraps are unique among advertising media because they are a one-time investment that continues generating impressions for 5–7 years with zero recurring costs. Compare this to digital advertising, where you pay per click, per impression, or per month — and the moment you stop paying, your visibility drops to zero.

The Outdoor Advertising Association of America (OAAA) has consistently ranked vehicle wraps as the most cost-effective advertising medium available to small and medium businesses. Their research shows that a single wrapped vehicle generates between 30,000 and 70,000 daily impressions depending on driving patterns and market density. In a metro area like Chicago, with 9.5 million people in the MSA, a wrapped fleet vehicle operating in commercial corridors can exceed 70,000 daily impressions.

Beyond raw impression counts, vehicle wraps benefit from what marketers call "forced exposure." Unlike digital ads that can be blocked, skipped, or scrolled past, a wrapped vehicle in traffic is unavoidable. Drivers and pedestrians cannot close a tab or change the channel — your brand message is delivered to every person within visual range of your vehicle. Studies show that 97% of people recall ads on wrapped vehicles, compared to just 19% for online display ads.

Long-Term ROI Analysis for Chicago Businesses

To properly evaluate any advertising investment, you need to calculate the cost per thousand impressions (CPM) over the lifetime of the campaign. For a typical full vehicle wrap costing $3,500–$5,000 with a 5-year lifespan, the math is compelling:

A wrapped vehicle generating 50,000 impressions per day produces 18.25 million impressions per year, or 91.25 million impressions over 5 years. At a wrap cost of $4,000, that translates to a CPM of $0.04 — four cents per thousand impressions. No other advertising medium comes close to this efficiency.

For Chicago businesses specifically, the density of the metro area amplifies these numbers. Vehicles operating on major corridors like I-90/94, Lake Shore Drive, Western Avenue, and Cicero Avenue encounter significantly higher traffic volumes than national averages. Service vehicles that make multiple stops per day in commercial and residential areas compound their impression counts as they park in visible locations throughout their routes.

Fleet wraps also generate what we call "compound brand equity." Unlike a digital ad that disappears after the campaign ends, a wrapped vehicle builds cumulative brand recognition in your service area over years. Customers who see your wrapped vehicles repeatedly develop familiarity and trust — the psychological foundation of brand preference. When they need your service, your company is the first name that comes to mind because they have seen your vehicles hundreds of times in their neighborhood.
