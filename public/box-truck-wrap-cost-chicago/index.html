Why Cast Vinyl is the Only Acceptable Commercial Wrap Material

The market for commercial vehicle wraps in Chicago faces a challenge regarding material quality. Many local shops offer wraps using calendered vinyl, a less expensive, extruded film priced at $4–6 per square foot. In contrast, Chicago Fleet Wraps utilizes cast vinyl, which costs $9.75 per square foot, for its box truck projects. While the initial lower cost of calendered vinyl might seem appealing for your box truck, the long-term performance and appearance will reveal the compromise.

The production method for calendered vinyl involves extruding liquid PVC under high pressure, a process that creates inherent molecular stresses within the film that are impossible to completely eliminate. These internal stresses are particularly problematic for box truck wraps in Chicago. Over time, and exacerbated by the city's harsh freeze-thaw cycles, corrosive road salt, and intense UV exposure, these stresses lead to material degradation, including shrinking, cracking, and peeling, diminishing the visual impact and longevity of your box truck's advertising.

For superior durability and aesthetic appeal on your Chicago box truck wrap, Chicago Fleet Wraps exclusively uses cast vinyl, specifically Avery Dennison MPI 1105 and 3M IJ180-CV3. This premium material is produced by pouring liquid PVC onto a flat surface and then curing it. This casting method entirely bypasses internal stress, resulting in a dimensionally stable film perfectly suited for the large, often complex surfaces of a box truck. It flawlessly conforms to curves and rivets, withstands Chicago's extreme weather conditions, and maintains its vibrant appearance, ensuring your box truck advertisement remains pristine for years.
