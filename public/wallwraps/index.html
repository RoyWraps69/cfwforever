Interior vs. Exterior Wall Wraps

Interior wall wraps ($8–$15/sqft) use standard cast vinyl with a gloss or matte overlaminate. Rated 5–7 years. Perfect for lobbies, retail interiors, restaurants, and offices. No sun exposure means longer life and sharper color retention. Most interior installs complete in 1–2 days.

Exterior wall wraps ($12–$22/sqft) use UV-resistant cast vinyl with anti-graffiti overlaminate. Rated 3–5 years depending on sun exposure. Ideal for brick, stucco, concrete, and metal exteriors. We specify different vinyl for sunlit walls versus shaded ones. Exterior projects often include pressure washing and primer application before install.

Surfaces We Wrap

  • Drywall (smooth, painted) — Standard install. No prep beyond cleaning. Best surface for detail graphics and photo murals.
  • Drywall (textured / knockdown) — Install possible with extended heat setting. Fine detail softens slightly but logos and blocks of color print well.
  • Brick and cinderblock — Requires specialized primer. We use Avery Dennison MPI 1405 Wall Film designed for porous masonry. Expect a 24-hour primer cure before install.
  • Concrete and polished concrete — Install after pressure-clean and primer. Rated 3–5 years on interior concrete walls.
  • Glass and windows — Use perforated window vinyl for storefronts (see-through from inside, full graphics from outside) or opaque frost for privacy graphics.
  • Metal (HVAC, equipment, roll-up doors) — Direct-apply with standard cast vinyl. Great for branded roll-up garage doors.
  • Tile and grouted surfaces — Case-by-case. Grout lines show through — design accordingly or use overlaminated textured vinyl.

Wall Wrap vs. Paint vs. Printed Canvas

Vinyl wall wraps deliver photo-quality detail with no brush strokes or texture limitations. Install in hours, not days. Remove cleanly when you rebrand. Cost $8–$22/sqft for most projects.

Painted murals can look stunning but require an artist, 3–14 days of labor, and disruption to your business. Repainting for a rebrand means starting over. Typical cost $15–$50/sqft.

Printed canvas or framed prints look like decoration, not branding. They hang from hooks, cast shadows, and limit design to rectangular panels. Wall wraps cover floor-to-ceiling, wrap corners, and feel permanent.

Frequently Asked Questions About Wall Wraps

How much do wall wraps cost in Chicago?

Wall wrap pricing depends on square footage and surface complexity. Simple drywall walls run $8–$15/sqft installed. Brick, concrete, and textured surfaces run $12–$22/sqft. Small feature walls start at $400, full-room murals range $2,200–$8,500.

How long do wall wraps last?

Interior wall wraps with Avery Dennison MPI 1105 cast vinyl are rated 5–7 years. Exterior-exposed installations run 3–5 years depending on sun exposure and weather. Indoor, controlled-climate installations regularly exceed 7 years.

Can you install wall wraps on brick or concrete?

Yes. We use Avery Dennison MPI 1405 Wall Film with specialized primers and adhesion promoters designed for porous masonry. A site visit confirms surface compatibility. Expect a 24-hour primer cure before install.

Will a wall wrap damage my wall?

No. When removed correctly, cast vinyl wall wraps pull cleanly off painted drywall, sealed concrete, and glass. On porous surfaces like raw brick, some primer residue may remain — we disclose this before installation and plan removal accordingly.

How long does wall wrap installation take?

Small feature walls install in 2–4 hours. Accent walls complete in a single day. Full-room murals typically take 1–2 days depending on complexity and surface prep. We schedule around business hours when clients need to stay open.

Can wall wraps be removed or updated?

Yes. Interior wall wraps on smooth surfaces remove cleanly in 2–4 hours. We can remove existing graphics and install new designs for rebrands, seasonal campaigns, or tenant turnover.

Do you handle design, or do I need to provide art?

Either works. Our in-house design team creates custom wall graphics based on your brand guidelines and space dimensions. If you have existing artwork (AI, EPS, high-res PDF), we can also print and install that directly.

What materials do you use?

Avery Dennison MPI 1105 cast vinyl for standard surfaces, MPI 1405 Wall Film for textured and porous surfaces, and 3M IJ180-CV3 for high-durability applications. All prints use HP Latex eco-solvent inks and DOL 1360 gloss or matte overlaminate.

Can you wrap windows?

Yes. We install perforated window vinyl (one-way view-through for storefronts), opaque privacy graphics, and static cling removable window displays. Storefront wraps start at $8/sqft.
