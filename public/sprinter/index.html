Sprinter Wrap Design Considerations

Sprinter vans have unique body lines that require specialized templates:

  • Sliding door integration — vinyl is cut along the door track so it opens and closes without catching
  • Rear door panels — twin or single rear doors with separate panels for each
  • High-roof transitions — seamless design flow from lower body to the extended roof panel
  • Wheel well contours — precise template cuts around complex wheel arch shapes
  • Bumper and trim integration — optional coverage for chrome or painted bumpers

Protecting Your Investment: Sprinter Van Wrap Care & Maintenance

Hand wash your wrapped Sprinter using mild, non-abrasive detergents and a soft cloth. Avoid harsh chemicals, abrasive brushes, or high-pressure washers near edges. Wash at least once a week on Chicago's busy roads. Promptly remove bird droppings, tree sap, or fuel spills — these can stain or degrade vinyl if left untreated.

Avoid automatic car washes. The harsh brushes, aggressive detergents, and high-pressure water jets commonly used can lift edges, scratch the vinyl, and significantly reduce your Sprinter wrap's lifespan. Stick to hand washing.

Apply a liquid wax or sealant designed for vinyl wraps every few months. This adds protection against UV rays, environmental contaminants, and minor abrasions — keeping your Sprinter wrap looking fresh and professional for the full 5–7 year lifespan.
