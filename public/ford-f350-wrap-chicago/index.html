Should I wrap my work truck or just put magnets on it?

Wrap it. Magnets fall off at highway speeds, look unprofessional up close, and only cover the doors. Can you wrap an F-350 with a service body?

Yes — we wrap F-350 and F-450 trucks with aluminum service bodies and utility boxes regularly. The service body is wrapped as part of the overall package. How long do truck wraps last on work trucks that take abuse?

Cast vinyl wraps on work trucks typically last 4–6 years depending on how the truck is used. Heavy off-road use, gravel roads, and frequent contact with brush or debris will shorten wrap life on specific panels. After the Wrap — Care, Warranty, and Reorders

Every Ford F-250, F-350, or F-450 Super Duty wrap comes with a comprehensive 2-year workmanship warranty, specifically covering issues such as lifting, bubbling, edge peeling, and any installation defects. This warranty is distinct from the manufacturer's vinyl warranty; both Avery Dennison and 3M offer their own film performance warranties, typically ranging from 5–7 years for outdoor use on these heavy-duty trucks.

To maximize the lifespan of your Ford Super Duty wrap, always hand wash with mild soap and water, and avoid automated brush car washes which can scratch and lift edges. Keep pressure washers below 1,200 PSI and at least 12 inches away from panel edges, and promptly address bird droppings and tree sap – these acidic compounds can etch into the vinyl if left too long.
