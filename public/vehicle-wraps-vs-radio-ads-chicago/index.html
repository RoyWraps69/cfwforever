Frequently Asked Questions

How do vehicle wraps compare to Google Local Service Ads?

Google Local Service Ads (LSAs) are intent-based — they reach people actively searching for your service right now. Vehicle wraps are awareness-based — they build brand memory before the need arises. Both are valuable. LSAs convert the immediate demand; wraps create the brand recognition that makes customers choose you over an unfamiliar competitor when that immediate need arises.

What is the ROI timeline for a vehicle wrap?

Most Chicago service businesses recover their wrap investment within 6–18 months through new customers who discovered them via the vehicle. The exact timeline depends on your service area, vehicle routes, and average customer lifetime value. A single new commercial account from a fleet manager who saw your wrapped van can pay for the entire wrap program.

Should I wrap my fleet before or after building my website?

Both matter, and both should be done. But if you have to choose one first, wrap your fleet. Wrapped vehicles generate brand impressions immediately and continuously in your physical service territory. Your website matters when people search online — but a wrapped van in their neighborhood drives search intent in the first place. They see the van, they search the company, they call.

Why Cast Vinyl is the Only Acceptable Commercial Wrap Material

The commercial vehicle wrap industry has a material quality problem. Walk-in shops across Chicago sell wraps using calendered vinyl — a cheaper extruded film that costs $4–6/sqft vs the $9.75/sqft Chicago Fleet Wraps charges for cast vinyl. The savings look attractive. The results are not.

Calendered vinyl is manufactured by extruding liquid PVC through a die under pressure. That process creates internal molecular stresses in the film that cannot be fully relieved. Over time — accelerated by Chicago's freeze-thaw cycles, road salt, and UV exposure — those stresses cause the material to shrink and pull away from panel edges. Wraps installed with calendered vinyl start lifting within 18–24 months on commercial vehicles. By year 3, they look visibly degraded.

Cast vinyl — specifically Avery Dennison MPI 1105 and 3M IJ180-CV3 — is manufactured by casting liquid PVC onto a flat substrate and curing it. This process eliminates internal stress, producing a dimensionally stable film that conforms to compound curves, survives extreme temperatures, and maintains appearance through 5–7 years of outdoor exposure. Chicago Fleet Wraps has never installed calendered vinyl on a commercial vehicle. We never will.
