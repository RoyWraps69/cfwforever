Why Cast Vinyl is the Only Acceptable Commercial Wrap Material

In the realm of commercial vehicle wraps, particularly for a specialized fleet like Rivian in Illinois, a significant issue with material quality persists. While many Chicago-based walk-in shops might entice Rivian fleet managers with calendered vinyl—a more economical, extruded film priced at $4–6 per square foot—Chicago Fleet Wraps exclusively uses premium cast vinyl, costing $9.75 per square foot. Though the initial savings on calendered options appear appealing for a large Rivian fleet, the long-term outcomes fall far short of expectations.

The production of calendered vinyl involves extruding liquid PVC through a die under high pressure. This manufacturing method inherently generates internal molecular stresses within the film that are impossible to completely eliminate. For Rivian fleets operating in Illinois, these stresses are dramatically amplified by Chicago’s harsh freeze-thaw cycles, corrosive road salt, and intense UV radiation, ultimately leading to premature degradation of the material.

Conversely, cast vinyl—specifically the top-tier Avery Dennison MPI 1105 and 3M IJ180-CV3—is produced by pouring liquid PVC onto a flat surface and then curing it. This superior method entirely bypasses internal stress, resulting in a film that boasts exceptional dimensional stability. This makes it ideally suited for the unique contours of Rivian vehicles, ensuring it adeptly handles extreme temperature fluctuations and retains its pristine appearance over time.

Each panel, from the front fender flares to the tailgate and charge port cover, demands individual attention. Our installers meticulously cut and apply premium Avery Dennison MPI 1105 or 3M IJ180-CV3 cast vinyl to each section, ensuring perfect alignment and adhesion, even on the vehicle's more challenging recessed areas and sharp angles.

The sheer number of individual components, coupled with the integrated cameras and sensors, necessitates a highly precise, panel-by-panel installation strategy.
