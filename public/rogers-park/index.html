Wrap Care and Maintenance Tips for for Rogers Park, Chicago Vehicle Owners

Protecting your investment starts with proper wrap care. Hand washing is always preferred over automated car washes. Use a mild automotive detergent (pH neutral, no wax additives) and a soft microfiber wash mitt. Rinse thoroughly with clean water and dry with a chamois or microfiber towel to prevent water spots.

If you must use an automated car wash, choose a touchless facility only. Brush-style car washes can lift edges, scratch laminate, and cause premature peeling. Avoid high-pressure washers aimed directly at wrap edges — keep the nozzle at least 12 inches from the surface and use a fan spray pattern rather than a pinpoint stream.

Chicago winters present unique challenges for wrapped vehicles. Road salt, brine, and sand can accumulate on wrap surfaces and cause staining if left for extended periods. We recommend washing salt residue within 48 hours of exposure. Store wrapped vehicles in a garage or covered area when possible to minimize UV exposure and temperature cycling that can stress adhesive bonds.

Our technicians examine every panel for edge lifting, adhesive failure, UV degradation, and impact damage. Early detection of minor issues prevents costly full-panel replacements and keeps your fleet looking professional year-round.
