Premium Materials for Chicago Weather

Chicago's climate is brutal on vehicle wraps. Temperatures swing from -10°F in January to 95°F+ in July. Road salt, ice scrapers, car washes, and UV exposure all attack vinyl film. That's why material selection is critical — and why Chicago Fleet Wraps exclusively uses premium cast vinyl:

Avery Dennison MPI 1105 is our primary film for commercial and fleet wraps. It's a 2.0-mil cast vinyl with Supreme Wrapping Film overlaminate, rated for 7+ years outdoor durability. The cast construction means it conforms to curves, rivets, and corrugations without shrinking or lifting — essential for cargo vans and box trucks with complex body panels.

3M IJ180-CV3 is our secondary film, a Controltac cast vinyl with 3M 8518 overlaminate rated for 5–7 years. The Controltac adhesive allows repositioning during installation, which means cleaner edges and fewer imperfections on difficult panels like bumpers and mirrors.

We never use calendered vinyl. Calendered film costs 40% less but shrinks, cracks, and fades within 1–2 years in Chicago weather. The $500–$800 you "save" on cheap vinyl costs you $3,000+ in premature replacement. Every wrap we install comes with a 2-year workmanship warranty and we maintain zero verified paint damage claims in 10+ years of operation.

Fleet Wrap Maintenance Tips for Rogers Park

Maintaining your vehicle wrap in Rogers Park is straightforward but important for maximizing its 5–7 year lifespan. Chicago's road salt, which is heavily applied on Rogers Park streets from November through March, is the single biggest threat to wrap longevity. We recommend washing your wrapped vehicle every 2 weeks during winter months using a touchless car wash or hand wash with mild soap and water.

Avoid automated car washes with brushes — the spinning bristles can lift edges and scratch the overlaminate. If you use a pressure washer, keep the nozzle at least 12 inches from the surface and use a wide fan pattern (40-degree tip), never a pinpoint stream. Water temperature should stay below 140°F. For stubborn road grime, use a dedicated vinyl wrap cleaner like Avery Dennison Supreme Wrap Care or 3M Wrap Care — never use petroleum-based solvents, abrasive compounds, or wax.

Parking matters too. If your Rogers Park business has indoor parking or a garage, use it. UV exposure is the primary cause of color fading, and every hour of shade extends your wrap's life. For vehicles parked outdoors in Rogers Park, we apply an additional UV-resistant overlaminate during installation at no extra charge. This extends color vibrancy by 1–2 years compared to standard overlaminate.
