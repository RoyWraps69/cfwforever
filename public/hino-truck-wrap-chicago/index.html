Cast Vinyl Materials — Why It Matters for Your Fleet

Chicago Fleet Wraps uses only two vinyl systems on commercial vehicle installs. Avery Dennison MPI 1105 is our primary fleet material — a conformable cast film rated 5–7 years outdoor with EZ RS adhesive that removes cleanly without damaging factory paint. 3M IJ180-CV3 is the other option — equally durable with Comply air-release technology that eliminates installation bubbles on smooth surfaces.

Neither of these is a cheap material. At $9.75/sqft installed, you are paying for films that outlast the competition by 3–5 years in real-world Chicago conditions — road salt, freeze-thaw cycles, UV exposure, and highway vibration. We have never had a verified paint damage claim in 10+ years of commercial installation. That record is only possible with premium cast vinyl applied correctly.

Every Hino box truck wrap we produce is finished with a DOL 1360 gloss overlaminate – a crucial clear protective layer. This addition provides robust UV protection, significantly enhances scratch resistance, and deepens the vibrancy of the printed graphics. Without this overlaminate, the printed vinyl on your Hino would deteriorate much faster under the intense Chicago sun. We strictly adhere to this step for all commercial installations.

After the Wrap — Care, Warranty, and Reorders

Each Hino box truck wrap comes with a robust 2-year workmanship warranty, specifically covering common issues such as lifting, bubbling, edge peeling, and any installation defects. This warranty is distinct from the manufacturer's vinyl warranty; both Avery Dennison and 3M offer their own film performance warranties, typically ranging from 5–7 years for outdoor usage on vehicles like your Hino.

To maximize the lifespan of your Hino truck wrap: always hand wash using mild soap and water, avoid automated brush car washes as they can scratch and lift edges, keep pressure washers below 1,200 PSI and at least 12 inches away from panel edges, and promptly address bird droppings and tree sap – these acidic compounds can etch into the vinyl if left for extended periods.
