Full Wrap vs. Partial Wrap

Full wraps cover the cab, both box sides, and the rear door for maximum impact and brand consistency. They transform a plain white truck into a rolling billboard that commands attention on every route.

Partial wraps focus on the highest-visibility areas — typically both box sides and the rear door — while leaving the cab and roof unwrapped. Partial wraps cost 40–60% less while still delivering thousands of daily impressions. They're ideal for businesses testing fleet branding or working with tighter budgets.

Rear door only is the most cost-effective option. Vehicles behind your truck at stoplights, in traffic, and at loading docks see your brand clearly. It's also the most common starting point for new fleet branding programs.

Box Truck Wrap Design

Box trucks have unique design considerations that generic wrap shops often miss:

  • Panel seams and corrugations — box bodies have vertical ribs that require precise template alignment
  • Roll-up door graphics — rear doors need special paneling to account for fold lines
  • Cab-to-box transition — seamless design flow from the cab to the box body
  • Readability at distance — large format means your text should be readable from 200+ feet
  • DOT/MC number placement — FMCSA-compliant lettering integrated into the wrap design

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a box truck wrap cost in Chicago?

A 16–18 ft box truck wrap costs $5,000–$7,800. A 24–26 ft runs $9,300–$13,000. All pricing includes design, cast vinyl, UV overlaminate, installation, and free pickup throughout Chicagoland.

How long does installation take?

2–3 days for a full box truck wrap. Design approval takes 2–5 business days. Delivery back same day or next day. Fleet jobs are batched for efficiency.

Can you wrap a leased box truck?

Yes. Cast vinyl is fully removable without paint damage. Leased fleet vehicles are one of the most common use cases. Wraps actually protect the factory paint.

What about corrugated box bodies?

We wrap corrugated, smooth, and FRP (fiberglass reinforced panel) box bodies. Corrugated bodies require additional surface prep and slightly more vinyl but produce excellent results.

Can you wrap a refrigerated truck?

Yes. Insulated and reefer box bodies are wrappable. The smooth FRP surface actually makes for easier installation than standard corrugated bodies.

Do you include DOT numbers?

Yes. FMCSA-compliant DOT and MC number lettering is integrated into your box truck wrap design at no additional charge. We ensure proper sizing and placement per federal requirements.

How long does a box truck wrap last?

Avery Dennison and 3M cast vinyl are rated 5–7 years outdoor. Box trucks with regular highway use typically see 5–6 years of sharp-looking graphics. UV overlaminate prevents fading and yellowing.
