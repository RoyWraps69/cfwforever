Fleet Graphics Installation — What to Expect

Our fleet wrap installation process is designed for minimal downtime and maximum consistency. Here's what happens from the moment you approve your fleet vehicle wrap design:

  • Vehicle Preparation — Each vehicle is thoroughly washed, degreased, and inspected for paint damage or body issues that could affect adhesion. Fleet vehicle wrapping requires a clean, smooth surface.
  • Climate-Controlled Installation — All fleet wraps are installed in our heated, dust-free bay at 4711 N Lamon Ave. Temperature and humidity are controlled to ensure proper vinyl adhesion — critical for Chicago's variable climate.
  • Professional Application — Our certified installers apply the fleet wrap graphics panel by panel, using heat guns and squeegees to ensure bubble-free, seamless coverage. Complex curves, rivets, and corrugations are handled with precision.
  • Post-Heat & Edge Sealing — After installation, every edge is post-heated to activate the adhesive's full bonding strength. This prevents lifting and ensures your fleet vehicle graphics last 5–7 years.
  • Quality Inspection — Multi-point inspection on every vehicle before release. We check alignment, edge adhesion, color consistency, and overall finish quality.
  • Aftercare Instructions — You receive written care instructions for maintaining your fleet wraps. Hand washing is recommended for the first 72 hours. After that, touchless car washes are safe.

Typical fleet wrap installation timelines: cargo vans 1–2 days, sprinter vans 2–3 days, box trucks 2–4 days, pickup trucks 1–2 days. We schedule fleet vehicles in rotation so your operations continue uninterrupted.
