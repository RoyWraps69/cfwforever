Why Contractors Wrap Their Fleets

Construction sites are the ultimate advertising platform. Your wrapped truck parked in front of a renovation project is seen by every neighbor, every passerby, and every delivery driver for the duration of the job. In Chicago's competitive contracting market, a branded fleet signals professionalism, legitimacy, and quality — helping you win bids over unmarked competitors charging the same rates.

Homeowners choosing between two contractors almost always pick the one with the branded truck. It's a simple trust signal that communicates: "This company is established, professional, and proud of their work." At $0.04–$0.07 per thousand impressions, fleet wraps deliver the lowest-cost marketing available to contractors.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much do contractor truck wraps cost?

Pickup truck wraps start at $2,200–$3,800, cargo vans at $2,800–$4,500, box trucks at $4,200–$10,000, and enclosed trailers at $1,500–$3,500. Fleet discounts of 5–15% apply for 3+ vehicles. All wraps are 100% tax deductible.

Why should contractors wrap their trucks?

Contractor trucks parked at construction sites advertise to the entire neighborhood for hours. A branded truck wins bids over unmarked competitors and generates referrals from every job site — at just $0.04 per thousand impressions.

Can you wrap trucks with toolboxes and racks?

Yes. We template every vehicle individually and cut wraps around toolboxes, ladder racks, headache racks, and other aftermarket accessories for a clean, professional finish.

How durable are wraps on construction vehicles?

Our wraps use premium cast vinyl rated for 5–7 years of outdoor durability. Even on trucks exposed to construction dust, debris, and frequent washing, the wrap maintains its appearance. Our 2-year installation warranty covers any issues.
