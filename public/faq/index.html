Materials & Quality

What vinyl material do you use?

We use only Avery Dennison MPI 1105 and 3M IJ180-CV3 premium cast vinyl — never calendered. Cast vinyl is thinner, more conformable to curves and rivets, and rated 5–7 years outdoor. See our Materials Guide for detailed specs.

What is the difference between cast and calendered vinyl?

Cast vinyl is poured into a mold and slowly cured, producing a thin, flexible, dimensionally stable film that lasts 5–7 years. Calendered vinyl is pressed through rollers — it is thicker, less conformable, and only lasts 2–3 years before shrinking and cracking. We exclusively use cast vinyl because it handles Chicago's -20°F to 100°F temperature range without lifting at panel edges.

What finishes are available for color change wraps?

We offer 120+ colors and finishes including gloss, matte, satin, metallic, chrome, color-shift, carbon fiber, brushed metal, and textured films. Visit our Color Change page or come to our shop to view physical samples.

Do you use laminate over the printed vinyl?

Yes. Every printed wrap receives a UV-protective over-laminate (Avery Dennison DOL 1460z or equivalent) that shields the graphics from UV fading, scratches, and chemicals. This extends the life of printed wraps by 2–3 additional years.

Paint Protection & Removal

Will a wrap damage my vehicle's paint?

No. Premium cast vinyl applied and removed correctly does not damage factory paint. Wraps actually protect the original paint from UV damage, rock chips, and minor scratches. Chicago Fleet Wraps has zero verified paint damage claims in 10+ years of commercial fleet operations.

Can a wrap be removed?

Yes. Cast vinyl wraps are designed for clean removal. Using controlled heat and proper technique, the vinyl peels away leaving the original paint intact. Professional removal takes 4–8 hours depending on vehicle size. See our Wrap Removal page for details and pricing.

Does the wrap protect against scratches and rock chips?

Vinyl wrap provides a sacrificial layer that absorbs minor scratches, rock chips, and road debris that would otherwise damage paint. It is not as thick as paint protection film (PPF/clear bra) but offers meaningful everyday protection, especially for fleet vehicles in commercial use.

Can you wrap a vehicle with existing paint damage?

Minor imperfections (small scratches, light oxidation) can usually be wrapped over. Deep scratches, rust spots, peeling clear coat, or body damage should be repaired before wrapping — the vinyl conforms to the surface and will telegraph any significant defects. We assess condition during vehicle intake.

Warranty & Durability

What warranty do you offer?

Every installation comes with a 2-year workmanship warranty covering lifting, peeling, bubbling, and adhesion failure caused by installation. The vinyl manufacturer provides a separate 5–7 year film warranty covering fading, cracking, and material defects. See our Warranty page for full details.

How do I maintain my wrap to maximize lifespan?

Hand wash with mild soap and water. Avoid automatic car washes with brushes. Do not use abrasive cleaners, solvents, or pressure washers above 2,000 PSI. Park in shade when possible. For detailed instructions, see our Wrap Care Guide.

Do wraps void my vehicle manufacturer warranty?

No. The Magnuson-Moss Warranty Act prevents manufacturers from voiding a warranty due to aftermarket modifications unless they can prove the modification caused the defect. We have wrapped 600+ Rivians and hundreds of Teslas with zero warranty issues. Vinyl wraps have zero effect on EV battery, charging, range, or electrical systems.

Can I wrap a leased vehicle?

Yes. Most leasing companies allow wraps since premium cast vinyl removes cleanly without damaging paint. We recommend checking your lease agreement, but in practice we wrap hundreds of leased vehicles per year with zero issues at lease return.

No. Automated brush car washes cause edge lifting and premature wear. Touch-free automated washes are acceptable. Hand washing is always preferred and extends wrap life significantly.
Can I pressure wash my wrapped vehicle?+
Yes, with caution. Keep pressure below 1,200 PSI, water temperature below 180°F, and nozzle at least 12 inches from the surface at a 40-degree angle. Never direct high pressure at seams or edges.
How often should I wash my wrapped vehicle?+
Weekly washing is recommended for commercial fleet vehicles. More frequent washing in winter to remove road salt from lower panels. Use wrap-safe, pH-neutral cleaners only.
Can I wax or polish a vinyl wrap?+
Do not use carnauba wax or petroleum-based polishes — they can cause discoloration. Vinyl-specific detailing sprays and ceramic coatings designed for vinyl are the correct products.
What products should I avoid using on a wrap?+
Avoid petroleum-based products, solvent cleaners, harsh degreasers, abrasive polishes, carnauba wax, and any cleaner not specifically labeled safe for vinyl wraps.
How do I remove bug splatter or bird droppings safely?+
Soak with a damp cloth for several minutes to soften, then gently wipe away. For stubborn residue, use a vinyl-safe citrus cleaner. Never scrub dry — you'll scratch the vinyl surface.
Can I use an ice scraper on the windows if they are wrapped?+
Only on glass surfaces that are not wrapped. Never use an ice scraper on vinyl-wrapped panels. For frost on wrapped surfaces, use a plastic squeegee with warm water.
Does a matte wrap require special soap?+
Yes. Use only matte-specific or vinyl-safe pH-neutral soap. Standard car shampoos can leave a sheen on matte finishes. Avoid spray waxes and any product that adds gloss.
How do I protect the wrap from UV damage?+
Park in shade or a garage when possible. Apply a UV-protective vinyl sealant designed for wraps. The laminate applied over CFW prints provides primary UV protection for the vinyl and ink.
What happens if I spill fuel on the wrap while filling up?+
Wipe immediately with a clean cloth. Fuel left on vinyl for extended periods can soften the adhesive and cause lifting. If staining occurs, use isopropyl alcohol on a clean cloth — do not scrub.

Legal, Insurance & Administrative

Do I need to notify the Illinois DMV if I change my car's color with a wrap?+
For a full color-change wrap that significantly changes the registered color, Illinois law technically requires updating the title. Most wrap owners do not update titles for temporary wraps. Consult your attorney.
Will my auto insurance cover the cost of the wrap if I'm in an accident?+
Standard auto insurance policies do not cover aftermarket wraps. You need to add the wrap value as an endorsement to your policy. Notify your insurer of the wrap value when it is installed.
Do I need to tell my insurance company I wrapped my car?+
Yes. Notify your insurance company and add the wrap value to your policy. Without disclosure, a claim for wrap replacement after an accident may be denied.
Are there restrictions on what I can advertise on my car?+
Advertising must comply with local ordinances. Explicit content and certain political messages may be restricted in some Chicago jurisdictions. CFW will advise on any obvious compliance concerns.
Are chrome wraps legal on public roads in Illinois?+
Chrome and mirror-finish wraps are legal in Illinois. Some jurisdictions have restrictions on highly reflective finishes that could blind other drivers. Check with your local municipality.
Can I wrap my windshield with a banner?+
No. Illinois law requires unobstructed visibility through the windshield. Any material applied to the windshield that impairs vision is illegal. CFW does not wrap windshields.
Does the wrap void my vehicle manufacturer's warranty?+
No. Under the Magnuson-Moss Warranty Act, a manufacturer cannot void a warranty solely because an aftermarket product was applied, unless they prove the product caused damage.
Is it legal to cover license plates or lights with vinyl?+
No. License plates must be fully visible and unobstructed at all times. CFW cuts around all plates, lights, and required markings during installation.
Do I need a permit for mobile advertising in downtown Chicago?+
Chicago does not require a permit for wrapped business vehicles operating normally. Stationary advertising vehicles used as static displays may require permits. Consult the City of Chicago's Business Affairs office.
What paperwork do I need to bring for the installation drop-off?+
Bring your vehicle title or registration confirming ownership. Fleet accounts should bring their account number. No additional paperwork is required for standard commercial wrap installations.

Removal & End of Life

How much does it cost to remove a wrap?+
Wrap removal ranges from $400 to $800 per vehicle depending on size and vinyl age. Cargo vans typically $400–$500. Box trucks $600–$800. Includes adhesive residue cleanup.
Can I remove the wrap myself?+
Yes, but proper technique is required. Use a heat gun at 140–180°F to soften the adhesive, then peel slowly at a low angle. DIY cold removal is the primary cause of paint damage. Adhesive residue cleanup requires IPA or commercial adhesive remover.
How long does the removal process take?+
Professional removal takes 4–8 hours for a cargo van and 8–12 hours for a box truck. Vinyl age is the biggest variable — older vinyl becomes more brittle and takes longer to remove cleanly.
When should I remove my wrap to ensure clean removal?+
Remove within the vinyl's rated lifespan — 5–7 years for cast vinyl. Removing within 6 years almost always results in clean, residue-free removal. Beyond 7–8 years, adhesive cures significantly.
Will the paint look brighter under the wrap than the exposed parts?+
Possibly. The wrap protects covered areas from UV fading. If your vehicle has significant sun exposure on unwrapped areas, there may be a color difference when the wrap is removed. This is expected, not a defect.
Can you recycle the old vinyl material?+
Vinyl wrap recycling is limited. Avery Dennison has a recycling program for their materials in some markets. Ask your CFW representative about current recycling options at time of removal.
What happens if the vinyl cracks before I remove it?+
Cracked vinyl is typically a sign of expired material or improper installation. Removal of brittle, cracked vinyl is more labor-intensive. CFW's 2-year labor warranty covers premature cracking from installation defects.
Do you guarantee clean removal if you didn't install it?+
CFW will perform professional removal on any vehicle but cannot guarantee results on wraps installed by other shops. See our Chicago wrap shop comparison to understand why material and installation quality vary significantly between shops. We assess vinyl condition and advise on expected results before starting.
Can I re-wrap the vehicle immediately after removal?+
Yes, in most cases. If the factory paint is in good condition after removal, the vehicle can be re-wrapped immediately with proper surface preparation. CFW does this routinely for fleet rebrand programs.
Does the age of the paint affect removal safety?+
Yes. Paint that is 15+ years old, heavily oxidized, or previously repainted may be more vulnerable during removal. CFW inspects paint condition before removal and advises on any elevated risk areas.
