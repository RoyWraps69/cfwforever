Cost & Pricing

How much does it cost to wrap a vehicle?

Pricing depends on vehicle size and coverage. Car wraps (sedans, coupes) start at $2,500–$3,500. Cargo van full wraps start at $3,150. Sprinter vans from $3,700. Box trucks 16–20 ft from $4,500. Box trucks 22–26 ft from $6,700. Bus wraps range $6,000–$15,000+. All pricing includes custom design, premium cast vinyl, professional installation, and a 2-year warranty. Use our Wrap Calculator for instant estimates.

Is a partial wrap cheaper than a full wrap?

Yes. Partial wraps typically cost 40–60% of a full wrap. A cargo van partial wrap (sides and rear) starts around $1,800–$2,400. Partial wraps are a smart option for businesses with tight budgets that still want high visibility on the road.

Do you offer fleet discounts?

Yes. We offer tiered fleet pricing: 5% off for 3–4 vehicles, 10% off for 5–9 vehicles, and 15% off for 10+ vehicles. For large fleet programs (30+ vehicles), we create custom pricing packages. Contact us for a fleet quote.

Are vehicle wraps tax deductible?

Yes. Commercial vehicle wraps qualify as advertising expenses under IRS Section 179 — 100% deductible in the year of purchase. A $3,150 cargo van wrap in the 28% federal tax bracket has an after-tax cost of approximately $2,268. Consult your CPA for your specific situation.

Is a vehicle wrap cheaper than a paint job?

A high-quality paint job with comparable coverage runs $5,000–$15,000+ for a commercial vehicle. A full vinyl wrap costs $2,500–$6,700 depending on size, is removable, and protects the original paint underneath. Wraps are typically 50–70% less expensive than equivalent paint and are reversible.

What is included in the price?

Every quote includes design consultation, custom artwork creation, one round of revisions, premium cast vinyl material (Avery Dennison or 3M), professional installation, post-install inspection, and a 2-year workmanship warranty. Pickup and delivery within our 50-mile service area is always free.

Materials & Quality

What vinyl material do you use?

We use only Avery Dennison MPI 1105 and 3M IJ180-CV3 premium cast vinyl — never calendered. Cast vinyl is thinner, more conformable to curves and rivets, and rated 5–7 years outdoor. See our Materials Guide for detailed specs.

What is the difference between cast and calendered vinyl?

Cast vinyl is poured into a mold and slowly cured, producing a thin, flexible, dimensionally stable film that lasts 5–7 years. Calendered vinyl is pressed through rollers — it is thicker, less conformable, and only lasts 2–3 years before shrinking and cracking. We exclusively use cast vinyl because it handles Chicago's -20°F to 100°F temperature range without lifting at panel edges.

What finishes are available for color change wraps?

We offer 120+ colors and finishes including gloss, matte, satin, metallic, chrome, color-shift, carbon fiber, brushed metal, and textured films. Visit our Color Change page or come to our shop to view physical samples.

Do you use laminate over the printed vinyl?

Yes. Every printed wrap receives a UV-protective over-laminate (Avery Dennison DOL 1460z or equivalent) that shields the graphics from UV fading, scratches, and chemicals. This extends the life of printed wraps by 2–3 additional years.

Paint Protection & Removal

Will a wrap damage my vehicle's paint?

No. Premium cast vinyl applied and removed correctly does not damage factory paint. Wraps actually protect the original paint from UV damage, rock chips, and minor scratches. Chicago Fleet Wraps has zero verified paint damage claims in 10+ years of commercial fleet operations.

Can a wrap be removed?

Yes. Cast vinyl wraps are designed for clean removal. Using controlled heat and proper technique, the vinyl peels away leaving the original paint intact. Professional removal takes 4–8 hours depending on vehicle size. See our Wrap Removal page for details and pricing.

Does the wrap protect against scratches and rock chips?

Vinyl wrap provides a sacrificial layer that absorbs minor scratches, rock chips, and road debris that would otherwise damage paint. It is not as thick as paint protection film (PPF/clear bra) but offers meaningful everyday protection, especially for fleet vehicles in commercial use.

Can you wrap a vehicle with existing paint damage?

Minor imperfections (small scratches, light oxidation) can usually be wrapped over. Deep scratches, rust spots, peeling clear coat, or body damage should be repaired before wrapping — the vinyl conforms to the surface and will telegraph any significant defects. We assess condition during vehicle intake.

Warranty & Durability

What warranty do you offer?

Every installation comes with a 2-year workmanship warranty covering lifting, peeling, bubbling, and adhesion failure caused by installation. The vinyl manufacturer provides a separate 5–7 year film warranty covering fading, cracking, and material defects. See our Warranty page for full details.

How do I maintain my wrap to maximize lifespan?

Hand wash with mild soap and water. Avoid automatic car washes with brushes. Do not use abrasive cleaners, solvents, or pressure washers above 2,000 PSI. Park in shade when possible. For detailed instructions, see our Wrap Care Guide.

Do wraps void my vehicle manufacturer warranty?

No. The Magnuson-Moss Warranty Act prevents manufacturers from voiding a warranty due to aftermarket modifications unless they can prove the modification caused the defect. We have wrapped 600+ Rivians and hundreds of Teslas with zero warranty issues. Vinyl wraps have zero effect on EV battery, charging, range, or electrical systems.

Can I wrap a leased vehicle?

Yes. Most leasing companies allow wraps since premium cast vinyl removes cleanly without damaging paint. We recommend checking your lease agreement, but in practice we wrap hundreds of leased vehicles per year with zero issues at lease return.

ROI & Business Value

How many daily impressions does a wrapped vehicle generate?

The Outdoor Advertising Association of America (OAAA) documents 30,000–70,000 daily impressions per wrapped vehicle. In Chicago's Cook County metro, service vans on regular residential routes typically generate 50,000+ daily impressions — approximately 87 million over a 6-year wrap lifespan.

What is the ROI of a vehicle wrap vs. digital advertising?

Vehicle wraps cost $0.04–$0.07 per 1,000 impressions vs. $6–$12 for Google Ads and $5–$8 for Facebook Ads. Over a 6-year lifespan, a $3,150 cargo van wrap generates 87+ million impressions — an ROI of 2,500–5,000%. Use our ROI Calculator for your specific numbers.

Does wrapping increase a vehicle's resale value?

Indirectly, yes. Wraps protect the original paint from UV damage, scratches, and wear. When the wrap is removed at resale time, the paint underneath is in better condition than an unwrapped vehicle of the same age. This can increase resale value by 10–15% compared to a vehicle with worn or sun-damaged paint.

How does a wrap compare to magnetic signs?

Magnetic signs are cheaper upfront ($200–$500) but cover only small areas, can scratch paint from trapped debris, blow off at highway speed, and look unprofessional. A full wrap covers the entire vehicle, generates 5–10x more impressions, and projects a premium brand image 24/7.

Chicago-Specific Questions

How do Chicago winters affect vehicle wraps?

Chicago's freeze-thaw cycles and road salt are tough on low-quality vinyl. Cast vinyl (Avery Dennison, 3M) handles -20°F to 100°F without shrinking. Road salt does not damage vinyl — just rinse it off promptly to prevent buildup in panel gaps. Our wraps regularly last 6–7 years through Chicago winters.

Is your shop climate-controlled?

Yes. Our installation bay is maintained at 70°F year-round. Proper temperature is critical for vinyl adhesion — cold shops cause lifting, hot shops cause overstretching. Our climate-controlled environment ensures consistent quality regardless of the season.

Do you need a permit to drive a wrapped vehicle in Chicago?

No. Wrapped vehicles do not require any special permits in Chicago or Illinois. Commercial vehicle wraps are classified as advertising and are fully legal on all roads and expressways. No city sticker, sign permit, or DOT filing is required for vinyl vehicle graphics.

Both are premium cast vinyl films rated for 5–7 years outdoor use. Avery Dennison MPI 1105 is our preferred film — it has excellent conformability on complex curves and rivets, and uses EZ RS (Easy Apply Removable Adhesive) for cleaner removal. 3M IJ180-CV3 is equally durable and is the standard specification for many fleet programs and government accounts. Both films are covered by manufacturer warranties and backed by our 2-year workmanship warranty. We never use calendered vinyl — it shrinks, lifts, and fails within 1–2 years.

What is the difference between a full wrap and a partial wrap?

A full wrap covers the entire painted surface of the vehicle — all four sides, hood, roof, and rear. A partial wrap covers specific high-visibility zones: typically both sides, the rear, or the rear three-quarters. Partial wraps deliver 60–80% of the visual impact of a full wrap at 40–60% of the cost. They are a smart choice when budget is a constraint or when the vehicle already has a solid base color that can be incorporated into the design. Full wraps provide complete paint protection and maximum branding coverage.
