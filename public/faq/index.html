Cost & Pricing

How much does it cost to wrap a car in Chicago?

A full car wrap in Chicago runs $3,200–$4,800 depending on the vehicle size and design complexity. Sedans and coupes are on the lower end, SUVs and crossovers on the higher end. This includes custom design, premium Avery Dennison or 3M cast vinyl, professional installation, and a 2-year workmanship warranty. Partial wraps and spot graphics start at $800–$2,500. How much does it cost to wrap a cargo van?

A full wrap on a standard cargo van (Ford Transit, Ram ProMaster, Chevy Express) costs $3,750–$4,500. This is the most common vehicle we wrap for commercial clients — HVAC companies, plumbers, electricians, and delivery fleets. The price includes design, premium cast vinyl, installation, and warranty. Partial van wraps (sides and rear only) run $1,800–$2,800.

How much does it cost to wrap a Sprinter van?

Sprinter and high-roof van full wraps cost $4,700–$5,500 due to the additional surface area and height. Mercedes Sprinters, Ford Transit high-roof, and Ram ProMaster high-roof all fall in this range. How much does it cost to wrap a box truck?

Box truck wraps depend on the truck size. A 16–20 ft box truck full wrap costs $5,000–$6,500. A 22–26 ft box truck runs $6,500–$8,500. The flat sides of box trucks are actually easier to wrap than curved vans, but the sheer surface area drives the cost. See our box truck wrap page for pricing details and examples.

Is it cheaper to wrap a car or paint it?

Wrapping is significantly cheaper. A quality commercial paint job runs $5,000–$15,000+. A full vinyl wrap costs $3,200–$6,500 depending on vehicle size — that is 30–50% less than paint. But cost is not the only advantage: wraps protect the original paint, are fully removable, and can be changed when you rebrand. Paint is permanent and can hurt resale value if the color is non-standard. For commercial vehicles, wraps win on every metric.

Are vehicle wraps tax deductible?

Yes. Commercial vehicle wraps qualify as advertising expenses under IRS Section 179 — 100% deductible in the year of purchase. A $4,000 van wrap in the 28% federal tax bracket has an after-tax cost of approximately $2,880. This makes wraps one of the most cost-effective marketing investments available to small businesses. Consult your CPA for your specific situation.

Paint, Damage & Removal

Will a vehicle wrap damage my paint?

No. Premium cast vinyl (Avery Dennison, 3M) applied and removed correctly does not damage factory paint. The wrap actually protects paint from UV fading, rock chips, and road debris. Chicago Fleet Wraps has completed 9,400+ wraps with zero verified paint damage claims. The key factors are: using cast (not calendered) vinyl, professional installation, and professional removal. Aftermarket paint, Maaco-type repaints, and touch-up paint may react differently — we assess this during vehicle intake.

What is the downside of wrapping a car?

Honest answer: wraps are not permanent and do require some care. The main considerations are: (1) Wraps last 5–7 years, not forever — they will eventually need replacement. (2) You cannot use automatic brush car washes. (3) Wraps will not hide existing paint damage — dents, rust, and peeling clear coat show through. (4) Low-quality wraps (calendered vinyl from budget shops) can damage paint and look terrible within a year. The solution to #4 is using a reputable shop that uses cast vinyl — which is what we do exclusively.

Can a wrap be removed? How much does removal cost?

Yes, wraps are designed for clean removal. Using controlled heat (heat gun) and proper technique, the vinyl peels away leaving the original paint intact. Professional removal costs $500–$1,500 depending on vehicle size and the condition of the existing wrap. Older wraps (7+ years) and wraps that were installed poorly take longer to remove. We offer removal as a standalone service — you do not need to be getting a new wrap.

Can you wrap a vehicle with existing paint damage, rust, or dents?

It depends on the severity. Minor scratches and light oxidation can usually be wrapped over. However, rust, peeling clear coat, deep scratches, and body damage should be repaired first. Vinyl conforms to the surface underneath — it does not fill or hide imperfections, it telegraphs them. We inspect every vehicle during intake and will let you know if any bodywork is needed before wrapping.

Does the wrap protect against scratches and rock chips?

Yes, to a degree. Vinyl wrap acts as a sacrificial layer that absorbs minor scratches, rock chips, and road debris that would otherwise damage paint. It is not as thick as dedicated paint protection film (PPF/clear bra), but it provides meaningful everyday protection — especially valuable for fleet vehicles in commercial use. For maximum protection, we also offer ceramic coating over wraps.

Materials & Quality

What vinyl material do you use?

We use only Avery Dennison MPI 1105 and 3M IJ180-CV3 premium cast vinyl — never calendered. Every printed wrap receives Avery Dennison DOL 1460 UV-protective overlaminate. Cast vinyl is thinner, more conformable to curves and rivets, and rated 5–7 years outdoor. We do not use budget vinyl, ever. See our Materials Guide for detailed specs.

What is the difference between cast and calendered vinyl?

Cast vinyl is poured into a mold and slowly cured, producing a thin (2 mil), flexible, dimensionally stable film rated 5–7 years. Calendered vinyl is pressed through rollers — it is thicker (3–4 mil), less conformable, and only lasts 2–3 years before shrinking, cracking, and lifting at edges. Calendered vinyl is cheaper, which is why budget shops use it. We exclusively use cast vinyl because it handles Chicago's -20°F to 100°F temperature range without failure.

Do you use laminate over the printed vinyl?

Yes, always. Every printed wrap receives a UV-protective overlaminate (Avery Dennison DOL 1460z) that shields the graphics from UV fading, scratches, road salt, and chemical exposure. This extends the life of printed wraps by 2–3 additional years compared to unlaminated prints. We offer both gloss and matte laminate finishes.

Wrap Care & Maintenance

Can a wrapped car go through a car wash?

Touchless car washes: yes. Automatic car washes with spinning brushes: no. The brushes can scratch the vinyl surface and lift edges over time. Hand washing with mild automotive soap and water is the best method and will keep your wrap looking new for years. See our Wrap Care Guide for detailed instructions.

How do I wash and maintain my vehicle wrap?

The basics:

  • Hand wash with mild soap (dish soap works) and water. Use a soft sponge or microfiber cloth.
  • Pressure washer is OK but keep below 1,200 PSI, hold 12+ inches from the surface, and spray at an indirect angle — never directly at seams or edges.
  • No wax on matte or satin finishes. Gloss wraps can be waxed with wrap-safe products.
  • Bird droppings, tree sap, fuel spills — clean within 24 hours to prevent staining.
  • Winter salt — rinse off road salt regularly. It will not damage the vinyl but can build up in panel gaps.

How do I protect my vehicle wrap in winter?

Chicago winters are hard on everything, but wraps handle them well. Key tips: (1) Rinse road salt off weekly — it does not damage vinyl but buildup in panel gaps can cause lifting. (2) Never use ice scrapers on wrapped surfaces — use a soft snow brush. (3) Avoid automatic brush car washes (this applies year-round). (4) If possible, park in a garage. (5) Our cast vinyl is rated for -20°F and handles Chicago freeze-thaw cycles without shrinking. We have 24+ years of wraps surviving Chicago winters.

Do wraps fade in the sun?

Quality wraps with UV-protective overlaminate resist fading for 5–7 years. The Avery Dennison DOL 1460 overlaminate we use on every printed wrap blocks UV radiation that causes color degradation. That said, a wrap parked in direct sun 12 hours a day in Arizona will fade faster than one garaged in Chicago. Parking in shade when possible extends the life of any wrap.

Warranty & Durability

How long does a vehicle wrap last?

Premium cast vinyl (Avery Dennison MPI 1105, 3M IJ180-CV3) is manufacturer-rated for 5–7 years outdoors. In Chicago's climate with proper care, our wraps regularly last 6–7 years. The overlaminate protects against UV fading, road salt, and chemical exposure. Calendered vinyl from budget shops lasts only 2–3 years — this is why material choice matters.

What warranty do you offer?

The vinyl manufacturer provides a separate 5–7 year film warranty covering fading, cracking, and material defects. If anything goes wrong with our installation within 2 years, we fix it at no charge. See our Warranty page for full details.

Do wraps void my vehicle manufacturer warranty?

No. The Magnuson-Moss Warranty Act prevents manufacturers from voiding a warranty due to aftermarket modifications unless they can prove the modification caused the defect. We have wrapped thousands of vehicles — including 600+ Rivians and hundreds of Teslas — with zero warranty issues. Vinyl wraps have zero effect on EV battery, charging, range, or electrical systems.

What happens if my wrap gets damaged?

Minor damage (scratches, small tears) can usually be repaired by replacing the affected panel — you do not need to redo the entire wrap. We keep your design files on record, so we can reprint and install a matching panel. Panel repairs typically cost $200–$600 depending on the area. For fleet clients, we offer maintenance packages that include annual inspections and panel replacements.

Does wrapping increase a vehicle's resale value?

Indirectly, yes. Wraps protect the original paint from UV damage, scratches, and wear. How does a wrap compare to magnetic signs?

Magnetic signs are cheaper upfront ($200–$500) but have significant drawbacks: they cover only small areas, can scratch paint from trapped debris, blow off at highway speeds, and look unprofessional. A full wrap covers the entire vehicle, generates 5–10x more impressions, projects a premium brand image 24/7, and doubles as paint protection. For any business serious about branding, wraps are the clear winner.

Leased & Financed Vehicles

Can I wrap a leased vehicle?

Yes. Most leasing companies allow wraps because premium cast vinyl removes cleanly without damaging paint. We wrap hundreds of leased vehicles per year with zero issues at lease return. Check your lease agreement to confirm, but in practice this is standard. When your lease ends, we can remove the wrap professionally for $500–$1,500 and the paint underneath will be in excellent condition.

Do I need to tell the DMV if I wrap my car?

In Illinois, no. You do not need to notify the Secretary of State (Illinois DMV equivalent) about a vinyl wrap. Vehicle wraps are classified as removable aftermarket modifications, not a permanent color change. Your vehicle registration and title remain unchanged. No special permits, city stickers, or DOT filings are required for vinyl vehicle graphics in Illinois.

Does my insurance need to know about my wrap?

It is a good idea to notify your insurance company and add the wrap's value to your policy. If the vehicle is in an accident, your standard auto insurance may not cover the cost of replacing the wrap unless it is listed as an addition. The cost to add wrap coverage is typically minimal — a few dollars per month. Keep your wrap invoice as documentation of the value.

Do you need a permit to drive a wrapped vehicle in Chicago?
