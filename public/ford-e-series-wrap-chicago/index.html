Can you wrap an older Ford E-Series van?

Yes — we wrap E-Series vans regularly. Ford discontinued the model in 2014 but thousands remain in active commercial service in Chicago. We inspect the paint condition first. How does an E-Series wrap compare to a Transit wrap?

E-Series vans have flatter, simpler panels than the Transit — which actually makes installation slightly simpler. The larger, flatter canvas also displays graphics with fewer interruptions. The tradeoff is that the E-Series has a dated profile compared to the Transit's more modern look.

After the Wrap — Care, Warranty, and Reorders

Every Ford E-Series wrap we install comes with a 2-year workmanship warranty, covering issues such as lifting, bubbling, edge peeling, and installation defects. This is distinct from the vinyl manufacturer's warranty; both Avery Dennison and 3M offer their own film performance warranties, typically ranging from 5–7 years for outdoor use on commercial vehicles like the E-Series.

To maximize the lifespan of your Ford E-Series wrap: always hand wash with mild soap and water, avoid automated brush car washes which can scratch and lift edges, keep pressure washers below 1,200 PSI and at least 12 inches from panel edges, and promptly address bird droppings and tree sap as their acidic compounds can etch into the vinyl if left for an extended period.

When you expand your Ford E-Series fleet with additional vehicles months or even years down the line, your branding files are securely stored with us. We maintain permanent records of all fleet artwork. This means reorders for your new Ford E-Series vans go into production much faster, as design approval is already established; we simply adapt your existing brand standards to the new E-Series vehicle template and submit it for your quick review.
