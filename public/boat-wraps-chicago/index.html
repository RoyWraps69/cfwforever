Why Wrap Your Boat Instead of Painting?

Marine paint jobs are expensive ($10,000–$30,000+), take weeks to complete, and commit you to a single look. Boat wraps cost $3,000–$8,000, install in 2–3 days, protect the original gelcoat underneath, and can be removed or changed whenever you want a fresh look. For charter companies and commercial operators, a wrapped boat is a floating billboard generating visibility at marinas, on the lake, and in social media photos.

Chicago's boating season runs May through October, and every weekend on Lake Michigan is prime advertising real estate. A professionally wrapped charter boat or commercial vessel generates thousands of impressions at Monroe Harbor, Burnham Harbor, and marinas across Chicagoland.

  • Boat Wrap — $3,000–$8,000 · 2–3 day install · Removable · Protects gelcoat

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a boat wrap cost in Chicago?

Boat wraps range from $300 for transom graphics to $8,000+ for full hull wraps on larger vessels. Pricing depends on boat length, hull complexity, and design. Free estimates available.

How long do boat wraps last?

Marine-grade vinyl boat wraps are rated for 5–7 years with proper care. UV-resistant overlaminate protects against sun, salt, and freshwater exposure.

Is a boat wrap better than paint?

Boat wraps cost 40–60% less than marine paint, install in days vs weeks, protect the gelcoat, and can be removed or changed without hull damage.

Can you wrap any size boat?

We wrap boats from 16-foot center consoles to 50+ foot yachts. Hull wraps, deck graphics, transom lettering, and full color changes available for all boat types.
