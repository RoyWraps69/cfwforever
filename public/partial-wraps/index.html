Full Wrap vs. Partial Wrap

A full wrap covers 100% of the vehicle and delivers maximum brand impact. A partial wrap covers 40–70% of the vehicle — focusing on the areas most visible in traffic, at job sites, and in parking lots. Studies show partial wraps deliver 70–80% of the visual impact of a full wrap at significantly less cost.

Partial wraps are ideal for:

  • Small businesses entering fleet branding for the first time
  • Companies testing vehicle advertising before committing to full wraps
  • Vehicles that are only partially visible (parked against walls, in driveways)
  • Budget-conscious fleet rollouts that can be expanded later
  • Vehicles with complex body lines where full wraps aren't cost-effective
