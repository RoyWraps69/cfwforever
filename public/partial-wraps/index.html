Full Wrap vs. Partial Wrap

A full wrap covers 100% of the vehicle and delivers maximum brand impact. A partial wrap covers 40–70% of the vehicle — focusing on the areas most visible in traffic, at job sites, and in parking lots. Studies show partial wraps deliver 70–80% of the visual impact of a full wrap at significantly less cost.

Partial wraps are ideal for:

  • Small businesses entering fleet branding for the first time
  • Companies testing vehicle advertising before committing to full wraps
  • Vehicles that are only partially visible (parked against walls, in driveways)
  • Budget-conscious fleet rollouts that can be expanded later
  • Vehicles with complex body lines where full wraps aren't cost-effective

Understanding Partial Wrap Options: Half, Quarter, and Custom Designs

When considering a partial vehicle wrap, it's essential to understand the various coverage options available to best suit your marketing objectives and budget. A 'quarter wrap' typically covers about 25% of the vehicle's surface, often focusing on the rear or side panels, ideal for prominent logo placement and essential contact details. This option is popular for smaller vehicles like Nissan NV200s or even compact sedans used for delivery services around areas like Evanston or Oak Brook.

'Half wraps' extend coverage to approximately 50% of the vehicle, providing a larger canvas for more elaborate graphics, brand messaging, and impactful imagery. This could involve wrapping the entire rear section of a Mercedes-Benz Sprinter van or combining side panel coverage with a prominent hood graphic. Pricing for a quarter wrap might range from $800 to $1,800, while a half wrap typically falls between $1,500 and $3,500, depending on vehicle size and design complexity. These figures are estimates and a custom quote is always recommended after a consultation.

Partial wraps are significantly more budget-friendly, typically costing 30-60% less than a full wrap. For instance, a half wrap might range from $1,500-$3,500, while a full wrap for the same vehicle could be $3,500-$6,000+, offering a cost-effective alternative for impactful branding.
