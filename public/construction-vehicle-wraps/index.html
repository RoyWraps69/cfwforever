Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a commercial vehicle wrap last?

Cast vinyl wraps using Avery Dennison MPI 1105 or 3M IJ180-CV3 are rated for 5–7 years outdoor. With proper care — hand washing, no automated brush washes — Chicago fleet wraps regularly hit 6–7 years even with road salt and winter exposure.

Do wraps damage paint?

No. Chicago Fleet Wraps has zero verified paint damage claims in 10+ years of operation. Wraps actually protect factory paint from UV and minor scratches. When removed correctly, paint is preserved — often in better condition than unwrapped vehicles.

Our Commitment to Quality Materials

Material selection is the single most important factor in wrap longevity and appearance. Chicago Fleet Wraps exclusively uses premium cast vinyl films from Avery Dennison and 3M. We purchase directly from authorized distributors and maintain strict inventory rotation to ensure every roll of vinyl is within its manufacturer-specified shelf life.

Cast vinyl films (Avery Dennison MPI 1105, 3M IJ180-CV3) are manufactured using a liquid casting process that produces a thin, dimensionally stable film with no internal stress. This means the vinyl will not shrink, crack, or pull away from compound curves over its rated lifespan of 5–7 years. The air-release adhesive channels in these films allow our installers to achieve bubble-free results even on complex body panels with deep recesses and compound curves.

We pair our print vinyl with premium protective overlaminates (Avery Dennison DOL 1460Z or 3M 8519) that provide UV protection, scratch resistance, and chemical resistance against road salt, bird droppings, tree sap, and industrial fallout. These overlaminates maintain their optical clarity and gloss level for the full rated lifespan of the vinyl, ensuring your fleet looks professional year after year.
