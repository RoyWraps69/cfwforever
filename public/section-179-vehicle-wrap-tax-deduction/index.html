Vehicle Wraps Are a Fully Deductible Advertising Expense

The IRS classifies vehicle wraps as advertising — not a capital improvement — which means the full cost is deductible as a business expense in the year of purchase. A cargo van wrap at $3,150 costs a business in the 24% bracket approximately $2,850 after the deduction. A 10-van fleet at $37,500 generates more than $9,000 in tax savings.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I deduct a vehicle wrap under Section 179?

Yes. Vehicle wraps are advertising expenses — fully deductible in the year the wrap is placed in service.

Does a vehicle wrap qualify as a capital expense or advertising expense?

The IRS classifies vehicle wraps as advertising, not capital improvements. Advertising is fully deductible in the year incurred.

What documentation do I need?

Keep your Chicago Fleet Wraps invoice (itemized materials and labor), proof of payment, and business vehicle use documentation.
