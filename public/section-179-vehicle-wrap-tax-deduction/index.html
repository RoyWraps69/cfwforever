Section 179 Vehicle Wrap Tax Deduction

Deduct 100% of your commercial vehicle wrap cost in the year it's installed.

Vehicle Wraps Are a Fully Deductible Advertising Expense

The IRS classifies vehicle wraps as advertising — not a capital improvement — which means the full cost is deductible as a business expense in the year of purchase. This is one of the most overlooked tax advantages available to small business owners and fleet operators.

A cargo van wrap at $3,750 costs a business in the 24% bracket approximately $2,850 after the deduction. A 10-van fleet at $37,500 generates more than $9,000 in tax savings.

How the Deduction Works

Full-Year Deduction

Unlike capital improvements that depreciate over 5–7 years, advertising expenses are deducted in full in the year incurred. Your wrap installed in December 2025 is fully deductible on your 2025 return.

IRS Classification

The IRS has ruled in Tax Court cases that vehicle wraps are advertising expenses. The wrap does not add to the vehicle's basis or useful life — it advertises your business.

Fleet Multiplier

Every vehicle in your fleet gets the same deduction. 5 vans at $3,750 each = $18,750 deductible. At 32% bracket, that's $6,000 in tax savings in one year.

Documentation

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I deduct a vehicle wrap under Section 179?

Yes. Vehicle wraps qualify as a fully deductible advertising expense. The IRS classifies vehicle wraps as advertising, not a capital improvement, allowing businesses to deduct the full cost in the year of purchase.

How much can I deduct for a vehicle wrap?

You can deduct the full cost of the wrap as a business advertising expense in the tax year it is placed in service. For a cargo van wrap at $3,750 in the 24% bracket, the effective after-tax cost is approximately $2,850.

Does a vehicle wrap qualify as a capital expense or advertising expense?

The IRS has ruled that vehicle wraps are advertising expenses, not capital improvements. Advertising expenses are fully deductible in the year incurred, while capital improvements must be depreciated.
