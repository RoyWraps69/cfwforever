Why Cast Vinyl is the Only Acceptable Commercial Wrap Material

The market for commercial vehicle wraps, particularly for Sprinter vans, often faces issues with material quality. Many Chicago-area shops may offer seemingly attractive prices for Sprinter van wraps by using calendered vinyl, a less expensive extruded film priced around $4–6 per square foot. This contrasts sharply with the $9.75 per square foot Chicago Fleet Wraps charges for superior cast vinyl, a difference that, while offering initial savings, often leads to unsatisfactory long-term performance on a Mercedes Sprinter.

Calendered vinyl, commonly used for less durable Sprinter van graphics, is produced by forcing liquid PVC through a die under high pressure. This manufacturing method embeds internal molecular stresses within the film that are impossible to completely remove. As time progresses, especially when a Sprinter van wrap endures Chicago’s harsh weather conditions like freeze-thaw cycles, corrosive road salt, and intense UV radiation, these inherent stresses cause the wrap material to degrade prematurely, leading to cracking, shrinking, and peeling.

Conversely, the optimal choice for a Mercedes Sprinter van wrap, cast vinyl — specifically Avery Dennison MPI 1105 and 3M IJ180-CV3 — is made by pouring liquid PVC onto a flat surface and then curing it. This distinct manufacturing method effectively eliminates internal stresses, resulting in a dimensionally stable film perfectly suited for the complex curves and contours of a Sprinter van. This material excels at withstanding extreme Chicago temperatures and maintaining its vibrant appearance and integrity for years.
