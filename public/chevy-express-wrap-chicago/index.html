Frequently Asked Questions — Chevy Express / GMC Savana Wraps Chicago

How much does a Chevy Express or GMC Savana wrap cost?

A Chevy Express or GMC Savana full wrap starts at $3,750 for standard wheelbase. Extended models run $3,950–$4,200. Their flat panel construction makes them one of the most straightforward commercial vans to wrap cleanly, keeping installation time and cost lower than vans with complex curves.

What is the difference between wrapping an Express and a Transit?

The Express has flatter, more traditional van panels while the Transit has more sculpted bodywork. Express wraps tend to be slightly less complex because the flat surfaces require less contouring. However, the Transit's more modern profile often results in crisper graphic presentation from a distance.

Do you wrap both Chevy Express and GMC Savana?

Yes — the Express and Savana share the same platform with minor cosmetic differences. We wrap both identically. If you run a mixed fleet with both GM vans, we match them precisely to the same brand standard across both nameplates.

Can you wrap an older Chevy Express?

Yes, provided the paint is in adequate condition. We inspect every vehicle before installation. Older Express vans often have oxidized paint or surface rust that needs to be addressed before wrapping. We will give you an honest assessment — if the surface isn't ready, wrapping it would be a waste of your money.

After the Wrap — Care, Warranty, and Reorders

Every Chevy Express and GMC Savana wrap comes with a robust 2-year workmanship warranty, covering issues such as lifting, bubbling, edge peeling, and any installation-related defects. This is distinct from the manufacturer's vinyl warranty, with Avery Dennison and 3M providing their own film performance guarantees of 5–7 years for outdoor use on your cargo van.

To maximize the lifespan of your Chevy Express or GMC Savana wrap, we recommend hand washing with mild soap and water. Avoid automated brush car washes, as they can scratch the vinyl and lift edges. When using pressure washers, maintain a distance of at least 12 inches from panel edges and keep the PSI below 1,200. Immediately address bird droppings and tree sap, as these acidic compounds can etch into the vinyl if left untreated on your cargo van.

This ensures that reorders for new cargo van wraps go into production significantly faster, as design approval is already established.
