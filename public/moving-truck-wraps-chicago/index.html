Why Moving Companies Need Truck Wraps

Moving trucks spend all day in residential neighborhoods — the exact places where future customers live. A professionally wrapped moving truck parked in a driveway for 4–8 hours during a move generates hundreds of close-range impressions from neighbors, dog walkers, and passing cars. Chicago has over 100,000 residential moves per year, and a branded truck builds instant recognition and trust.

Unlike digital ads that disappear when you stop paying, a wrapped moving truck works 24/7 — on the highway, at storage facilities, and parked at your yard. At $0.04 CPM, vehicle wraps deliver the lowest cost-per-impression of any advertising medium. Many moving companies report that a single wrapped truck generates 3–5 direct leads per week.

Moving Truck Wrap Design Best Practices

The most effective moving truck wraps feature company name visible from 200+ feet (box truck sides are massive canvases), a phone number in the largest font possible, your website and Google review rating, and 2–3 key service areas. Include your license/DOT numbers for credibility. Bold, clean designs with high contrast outperform cluttered layouts — your truck is moving at speed and needs to communicate in seconds. Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a moving truck wrap cost in Chicago?

Moving truck wraps range from $5,000–$10,900 for box trucks (16–26 ft). Cargo van wraps start at $2,800–$4,500. Fleet discounts of 5–15% apply for 3+ vehicles.

How long does a moving truck wrap last?

Premium cast vinyl wraps are rated for 5–7 years. Quality installation with Avery Dennison or 3M materials ensures durability through heavy daily use, loading/unloading, and highway driving.

Can you wrap a fleet of moving trucks?

Absolutely. We specialize in fleet rollouts and can wrap multiple trucks on a phased schedule to minimize downtime. Fleet discounts apply for 3+ vehicles.

Do moving truck wraps protect the truck body?

Yes. Vinyl wraps act as a protective layer against minor scratches, stone chips, and UV fading. When removed, the original paint underneath is preserved.
