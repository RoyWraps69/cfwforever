Cast Vinyl vs Calendered Vinyl — The Material Difference That Determines Wrap Life

Cast vinyl is manufactured by casting liquid PVC onto a flat surface and curing it — this eliminates internal stresses and creates a dimensionally stable film that conforms to curves and survives 5–7 years. Calendered vinyl is extruded under pressure — it retains internal stresses that cause shrinkage, lifting, and premature failure on curved surfaces and in temperature extremes.

The Chicago Service Business Case

Calendered vinyl wraps fail predictably: edges start lifting within 18–24 months, especially at bumpers, door handles, and curved surfaces. The material shrinks in cold Chicago winters, pulling away from panel edges. For a commercial fleet vehicle that needs to look professional for 5+ years, calendered vinyl is a false economy — you pay less per sqft but replace the wrap 2–3 times in the same period.
