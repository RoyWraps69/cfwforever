Why Cast Vinyl is the Only Acceptable Commercial Wrap Material

The market for commercial vehicle wraps, particularly for a Ford Transit in Chicago, is plagued by inconsistent material quality. Numerous local shops offer wraps made with calendered vinyl, an extruded film that's significantly cheaper, often retailing for $4–6 per square foot. In contrast, Chicago Fleet Wraps uses cast vinyl, priced at $9.75 per square foot. While the initial cost savings of calendered vinyl might seem appealing for your Ford Transit, the long-term performance and aesthetic outcomes are often subpar.

Calendered vinyl, frequently used for less expensive Ford Transit wraps, is produced by forcing liquid PVC through a die under high pressure. This manufacturing method introduces inherent molecular stresses within the film that are impossible to completely eliminate. As time progresses, especially when subjected to Chicago's harsh weather conditions, including repeated freeze-thaw cycles, corrosive road salt, and intense UV radiation, these internal stresses inevitably lead to the material's premature degradation, making it an unsuitable choice for a durable Transit van wrap.

For a robust and long-lasting Ford Transit wrap in Chicago, cast vinyl — specifically our preferred Avery Dennison MPI 1105 and 3M IJ180-CV3 — offers superior performance. This material is created by pouring liquid PVC onto a flat surface and then curing it, a process that inherently removes internal stresses. The resulting film is exceptionally dimensionally stable, allowing it to seamlessly conform to the intricate curves of a Transit van, withstand extreme temperatures, and maintain its vibrant appearance and integrity for years, ensuring your investment pays off.
