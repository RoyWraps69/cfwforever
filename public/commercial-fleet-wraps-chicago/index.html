Fleet Services

What Are Commercial Fleet Wraps?

Commercial fleet wraps are vinyl graphics applied to multiple company vehicles to create a unified brand presence on the road. Unlike single-vehicle wraps or personal car wraps, commercial fleet wraps require exact consistency across every vehicle in the fleet — same colors, same logo placement, same messaging, same quality. When a customer sees your van in Lincoln Park and your box truck in Naperville, both vehicles must look like they belong to the same professional operation.

A commercial fleet wrap typically includes your company logo, business name, phone number, website, services offered, and any required licensing or certification information. The design is adapted to each vehicle model in your fleet while maintaining visual consistency. A cargo van wrap and a box truck wrap will have different dimensions but identical branding impact.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions About Commercial Fleet Wraps

How much do commercial fleet wraps cost in Chicago?

Commercial fleet wraps range from $3,200 for a pickup truck to $10,900 for a large box truck. Fleet volume discounts of 5-15% apply for 3+ vehicles. A typical 10-vehicle cargo van fleet costs approximately $33,750 after the 10% fleet discount. All costs are 100% tax-deductible under IRS Section 179.

How long do commercial fleet wraps last?

Commercial fleet wraps installed with Avery Dennison MPI 1105 cast vinyl last 5-7 years with proper care. Our 2-year workmanship warranty covers any installation defects. The vinyl manufacturer's warranty covers material failure for the full rated lifespan.

Can you wrap different vehicle types to look consistent?

Yes. We create model-specific design templates that maintain brand consistency across cargo vans, box trucks, sprinter vans, and pickup trucks. The logo placement, text sizing, and graphic proportions are calibrated per model so your fleet looks unified regardless of vehicle type.

Do commercial fleet wraps damage the vehicle paint?

No. Professional-grade cast vinyl actually protects the paint underneath from UV damage, minor scratches, and road debris. When removed properly (which we offer as a service), the paint underneath is in better condition than exposed paint. This preserves resale value.
