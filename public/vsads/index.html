Cost-Per-Impression: A Key Differentiator in Chicago's Market

When analyzing return on investment, the cost-per-impression (CPI) is a critical metric. Google Ads operates on a pay-per-click (PPC) model, where you pay for every interaction, which can quickly add up. Vehicle wraps, however, offer an incredibly low CPI over their lifespan, often reaching pennies or even fractions of a penny per thousand views.

Consider a full wrap for a standard cargo van, which might range from $3,500 to $5,500 depending on complexity. Spread over a 5-7 year lifespan, and considering the thousands of daily impressions in a dense urban environment like Chicago, the long-term value becomes exceptionally clear compared to ongoing PPC campaigns.

While Google Ads can deliver immediate, targeted traffic, the cost to maintain that visibility is continuous. Once your budget runs out, your ads disappear. Vehicle wraps, conversely, are a one-time investment that continues to generate impressions for years without additional monthly fees.

This sustained, low-cost visibility is particularly advantageous for small to medium-sized businesses in Chicago aiming to establish a strong, enduring brand without the perpetual expenditure of digital advertising. It’s an asset that depreciates slower than many digital campaigns.
