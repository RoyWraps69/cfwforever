Why Cast Vinyl is the Only Acceptable Commercial Wrap Material

A significant issue within the commercial vehicle wrap sector revolves around material integrity. Many Chicago-based walk-in shops frequently offer wraps utilizing calendered vinyl – a less expensive, extruded film that typically costs $4–6 per square foot. In contrast, Chicago Fleet Wraps employs cast vinyl, priced at $9.75 per square foot, for projects like those for Arnold Electric. While the initial cost savings of calendered vinyl may seem appealing, the long-term outcomes are consistently subpar for fleet branding.

The production of calendered vinyl involves extruding liquid PVC under pressure through a die. This manufacturing method inherently creates internal molecular stresses within the film that are impossible to completely alleviate. Over time, these inherent stresses lead to material degradation, a process significantly accelerated by Chicago's harsh environmental conditions, including its frequent freeze-thaw cycles, corrosive road salt, and intense UV radiation, ultimately compromising the professional appearance of Arnold Electric's fleet.

For Arnold Electric's fleet, we exclusively utilize cast vinyl, specifically Avery Dennison MPI 1105 and 3M IJ180-CV3. This premium material is produced by pouring liquid PVC onto a flat surface and allowing it to cure. This meticulous casting process eliminates internal stresses, resulting in a dimensionally stable film perfectly suited for conforming to the complex curves often found on service vehicles, enduring Chicago's extreme temperatures, and maintaining its vibrant appearance and adhesive properties for years.
