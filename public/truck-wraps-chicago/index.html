Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions About Truck Wraps

How much does it cost to wrap a truck in Chicago?

Truck wrap pricing in Chicago depends on the truck size. Pickup trucks (F-150, Silverado, Ram 1500) cost $3,200 to $4,500 for a full wrap. Mid-size box trucks (14-16 ft) run $4,500 to $6,000. Large box trucks (20-26 ft) range from $5,500 to $8,000. Chicago Fleet Wraps includes custom design, premium 3M or Avery vinyl, UV lamination, and professional installation in every price.

What is the best wrap material for trucks?

3M IJ180Cv3 and Avery Dennison MPI 1105 are the industry-standard cast vinyl films for truck wraps. Cast vinyl conforms to rivets, corrugations, and compound curves on truck bodies without lifting. Chicago Fleet Wraps exclusively uses these premium materials — never calendered vinyl, which shrinks and cracks in Chicago's temperature extremes. Every truck wrap receives a UV-protective over-laminate for 5-7 year durability.

How long does it take to wrap a truck?

A full truck wrap takes 3 to 5 business days from vehicle drop-off to pickup. The timeline breaks down to: 1-2 days for surface preparation and cleaning, 1-2 days for vinyl application, and a final day for quality inspection and curing. For fleet orders of 5+ trucks, Chicago Fleet Wraps offers staggered scheduling so you never have your entire fleet offline at once.

Can you wrap a truck with an existing paint job?

Yes. Vehicle wraps are applied over factory paint and actually protect it from UV damage, rock chips, and minor scratches. When the wrap is removed after 5-7 years, the original paint underneath is in pristine condition. This is especially valuable for leased trucks — the wrap protects the paint and removes cleanly at lease end, avoiding damage charges.
