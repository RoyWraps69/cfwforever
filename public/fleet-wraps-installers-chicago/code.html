Why Certified Fleet Wrap Installers Matter

Fleet wraps are not the same as wrapping a single car. When you're branding 5, 10, or 50 vehicles, every wrap must look identical — same color density, same alignment, same edge finishing, same rivet treatment. A single inconsistent vehicle in your fleet undermines the entire investment. This is why certification matters.

Avery Dennison and 3M certification programs train installers specifically on commercial-grade vinyl application. Certified installers understand heat-forming techniques for complex curves, post-heating protocols that prevent edge lifting in temperature extremes, and panel sequencing strategies that ensure consistent appearance across different vehicle models in the same fleet.

Our lead installer, Roy Turk, has been installing commercial vehicle wraps since 2001. He has personally overseen the installation of more than 9,400 vehicles — from single cargo vans to 50-vehicle enterprise fleets. His experience means he's seen every panel configuration, every rivet pattern, and every installation challenge that Chicago's commercial vehicle fleet presents.

What Makes Our Installation Process Different

Climate-Controlled Installation Bays

Vinyl adhesive performance is directly tied to temperature and humidity. Below 60°F, adhesive becomes sluggish and doesn't bond properly. Above 90°F, it becomes too aggressive and prevents repositioning. Our three dedicated wrap bays maintain 68-72°F year-round — the optimal range for Avery Dennison MPI 1105 and 3M IJ180Cv3 application. Chicago's winters drop to -10°F and summers hit 95°F+. A shop without climate control is gambling with your wrap's longevity.

Surface Preparation Protocol

Before any vinyl touches your vehicle, we follow a 5-step surface preparation protocol:

  1. Hand wash — full exterior wash to remove road grime, salt, and debris
  2. Clay bar treatment — removes embedded contaminants that washing misses
  3. Isopropyl alcohol wipe — degreases every panel to ensure adhesive bond
  4. Rivet and seam inspection — identifies areas requiring primer or extra adhesion treatment
  5. Temperature equilibration — vehicle sits in the bay for 2+ hours to reach ambient temperature before application begins

This protocol takes 2-3 hours per vehicle. Many shops skip it entirely. The result of skipping surface prep is edge lifting within 3-6 months — the single most common wrap failure mode.

Post-Heat Protocol

After installation, every edge, curve, and recessed area is post-heated with a calibrated heat gun to 210-220°F. This activates the adhesive's memory and ensures the vinyl conforms permanently to the vehicle's contours. Without post-heating, vinyl will shrink back from edges in cold weather — a problem that's especially severe in Chicago's winters where temperatures swing 80+ degrees between seasons.

Fleet-Specific Installation Capabilities

Fleet wraps present unique challenges that single-vehicle wraps don't:

Color Consistency Across Vehicles

When you wrap 10 vehicles, all 10 must match exactly. We print entire fleet orders from the same vinyl lot and ink batch. We calibrate our HP Latex 570 printers before each fleet run and print color verification strips that are checked against Pantone standards. If a single panel doesn't match, we reprint it before installation begins.

Multi-Model Fleet Coordination

Most commercial fleets include multiple vehicle types — cargo vans, box trucks, and pickup trucks operating under the same brand. Each vehicle model has different dimensions, panel configurations, and surface geometries. Our design team creates model-specific templates that maintain brand consistency while adapting to each vehicle's unique shape. The logo placement, text sizing, and graphic proportions are calibrated per model so the fleet looks unified on the road.

Volume Scheduling

We can wrap 2-3 vehicles per day with our three-bay facility. A 10-vehicle fleet can be completed in 4-5 business days. A 20-vehicle fleet in 8-10 days. For larger fleets, we coordinate a rolling schedule — picking up 3 vehicles at a time, wrapping them, delivering them back, and picking up the next batch. Your fleet never goes fully offline.
