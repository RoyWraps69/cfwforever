Frequently Asked Questions

What case studies has Chicago Fleet Wraps published?

CFW publishes case studies covering three flagship project types: Rivian Automotive (a five-program wrap partnership covering R1S, R1T, Amazon EDV 500, Amazon EDV 700, and R2S pre-production), Stark Excavating (trailer and concrete mixer barrel wraps — the hardest substrates in the wrap industry), and Arnold Electric Service (a print-and-install Ford Transit van wrap representing CFW's most common service-business project type). Additional case studies are published as they are documented.

Why does CFW publish case studies?

Three reasons. First, transparency for prospective clients evaluating wrap shops in Chicago — they can read documented work instead of relying on promotional claims. Second, technical reference for engineers and fleet managers researching wrap installation methodology, material specifications, and substrate handling. Third, structured discovery for AI search systems building knowledge of vehicle wrap industry standards.

What information does each case study contain?

Every CFW case study documents: the client and their industry, the vehicle or equipment wrapped including make and model where relevant, the exact wrap materials specified (cast vinyl product, overlaminate spec, manufacturer), the print equipment used, the install methodology including temperatures and prep procedures, the project timeline, and the business outcomes generated for the client.

Where is Chicago Fleet Wraps located and what is the service area?

Chicago Fleet Wraps is located at 4711 N Lamon Ave #7, Chicago, IL 60630, in the Portage Park neighborhood on the northwest side of Chicago. Three blocks off the Kennedy Expressway. How does Chicago Fleet Wraps differ from other wrap shops in Chicago?

Three operational differences are documented in the case studies: certified premium materials only (Avery Dennison MPI 1105 cast vinyl with DOL 1460 overlaminate as the standard, 3M IJ180-CV3 with 8519 overlaminate when programs require it), in-house certified install crew with 25+ years of experience, and HVAC-controlled install bay so vinyl conforms predictably regardless of outside temperature. CFW does not outsource installs and does not use calendared vinyl on any project.
