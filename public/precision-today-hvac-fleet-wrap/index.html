Why Cast Vinyl is the Only Acceptable Commercial Wrap Material

The realm of commercial vehicle wraps frequently grapples with issues concerning material quality. Many local shops throughout Chicago offer wraps utilizing calendered vinyl – a more economical extruded film priced at $4–6 per square foot. In contrast, Chicago Fleet Wraps invests in cast vinyl for Precision Today HVAC’s fleet, which, at $9.75 per square foot, delivers superior, lasting results compared to the initial, seemingly appealing cost savings of lower-grade alternatives. Calendered vinyl production involves forcing liquid PVC through a die under high pressure. This technique instills internal molecular stresses within the film that are impossible to fully dissipate. Over time, these inherent stresses, exacerbated by Chicago's harsh climate with its freeze-thaw cycles, corrosive road salt, and intense UV radiation, inevitably lead to the degradation and failure of the wrap, compromising the professional appearance of Precision Today HVAC's vehicles. In contrast, the cast vinyl chosen for the Precision Today HVAC fleet, specifically Avery Dennison MPI 1105 and 3M IJ180-CV3, is produced by pouring liquid PVC onto a flat surface and then curing it. This method eliminates any internal stress, yielding a dimensionally stable film perfectly suited for conforming to the complex curves of HVAC service vehicles, enduring extreme temperatures, and maintaining its pristine appearance for years. Chicago Fleet Wraps conducts all operations for programs like Precision Today HVAC's fleet wrap at its specialized facility located at 4711 N Lamon Ave #7, Chicago, IL 60630. This state-of-the-art shop is fully equipped to handle a wide range of vehicle sizes, from compact cargo vans to large 26-foot box trucks, featuring a climate-controlled installation bay that ensures a consistent temperature for optimal vinyl adhesion and a flawless finish on every Precision Today HVAC vehicle.

Calendered vinyl is manufactured by extruding liquid PVC through a die under pressure. That process creates internal molecular stresses in the film that cannot be fully relieved. Over time — accelerated by Chicago's freeze-thaw cycles, road salt, and UV exposure — those stresses cause the material to shrink and pull away from panel edges. Wraps installed with calendered vinyl start lifting within 18–24 months on commercial vehicles. By year 3, they look visibly degraded.

Cast vinyl — specifically Avery Dennison MPI 1105 and 3M IJ180-CV3 — is manufactured by casting liquid PVC onto a flat substrate and curing it. This process eliminates internal stress, producing a dimensionally stable film that conforms to compound curves, survives extreme temperatures, and maintains appearance through 5–7 years of outdoor exposure. Chicago Fleet Wraps has never installed calendered vinyl on a commercial vehicle. We never will.
