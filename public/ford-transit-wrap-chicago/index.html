Does wrapping a Ford Transit void the warranty?

No. Vehicle wraps do not void manufacturer warranties in the United States. The Magnuson-Moss Warranty Act prohibits manufacturers from voiding warranties solely because of aftermarket additions like vinyl wraps. Ford's warranty covers mechanical and structural defects regardless of whether the vehicle is wrapped.

After the Wrap — Care, Warranty, and Reorders

Each Ford Transit wrap we install comes with a 2-year workmanship warranty, specifically covering issues like lifting, bubbling, edge peeling, and any installation-related defects. This warranty is separate from the manufacturer’s vinyl warranty; both Avery Dennison and 3M offer their own film performance guarantees, typically ranging from 5–7 years for outdoor use on your Ford Transit.

To maximize the lifespan of your Ford Transit wrap: always hand wash with mild soap and water, avoid automated brush car washes as they can scratch and lift edges, keep pressure washers below 1,200 PSI and at least 12 inches away from panel edges, and promptly address bird droppings and tree sap – these acidic compounds can etch into the vinyl if left on your Ford Transit for too long.

Even if you expand your Ford Transit fleet months or years down the line, your specific brand design files are permanently stored within our system. We never discard fleet artwork. Subsequent reorders for additional Ford Transit wraps enter production much faster, as the initial design approval is already complete; we simply adapt your established brand standards to the new Ford Transit vehicle template and submit for final approval.
