Color Change vs. Paint

A quality paint color change costs $5,000–$15,000+ and is permanent. A color change wrap costs $2,500–$7,500, lasts 5–7 years, and is fully reversible. The wrap protects your original paint — preserving resale value. When you're ready for a new color, we remove the old wrap and apply a new one. No sanding, no body work, no permanent commitment.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a color change wrap cost in Chicago?

$2,500–$5,500 for standard colors (gloss, matte, satin, metallic). Chrome and specialty finishes run $4,000–$8,000. Pricing depends on vehicle size and finish complexity.

How long does a color change wrap last?

Avery Dennison Supreme and 3M 2080 are rated 5–7 years outdoor. Hand wash only, avoid automated car washes with brushes.

Will a color change wrap damage my paint?

No. Color change wraps are fully removable without paint damage. They protect the factory paint from UV, salt, and scratches.

Can you wrap a Tesla or EV?

Yes. We've wrapped 600+ Rivians and hundreds of Teslas. We're Chicago's leading EV wrap shop. See our EV Wraps page.

Is a color change wrap cheaper than paint?

Yes. A quality paint job costs $5,000–$15,000+. A wrap costs $2,500–$7,500, is fully reversible, and preserves resale value.

How long does installation take?

3–5 days for a full color change. Simpler vehicles (sedans) are faster. Complex body lines (exotics, SUVs) take longer.

Can I see color samples in person?

Yes. Visit our shop at 4711 N. Lamon Ave to browse our sample library. We also offer vinyl swatches that you can compare against your vehicle.
