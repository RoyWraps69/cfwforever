Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions About Van Wraps

How much does a van wrap cost in Chicago?

Van wrap pricing in Chicago ranges from $3,200 to $5,500 for a full wrap depending on the van model. Compact cargo vans (Transit Connect, City Express) start at $3,200. Full-size cargo vans (Ford Transit, Ram ProMaster, Chevy Express) run $3,750 to $4,800. Extended-length high-roof vans (Sprinter 170, Transit 148 EL) cost $4,500 to $5,500. All prices include custom design, 3M or Avery vinyl, lamination, and installation.

What type of van wrap gets the most attention?

Full wraps with high-contrast color schemes generate the most impressions. Based on our data from 1,200+ van wraps, orange and black wraps receive 40% more brand recall than blue and white. Matte finishes on dark base colors generate 25% more social media shares than gloss. The most effective van wraps include a phone number at minimum 6-inch height, the company name at eye level on both sides, and a clear service description visible from 30 feet.

Do van wraps damage the paint?

No. Professional-grade cast vinyl wraps from 3M and Avery Dennison are designed to be removable without paint damage. The adhesive is pressure-sensitive, not permanent — it bonds firmly enough to stay in place for 5-7 years but releases cleanly with heat application during removal. Chicago Fleet Wraps has removed thousands of wraps with zero paint damage claims. Wraps actually protect factory paint from UV fading, rock chips, and minor scratches.

Can you wrap a van with sliding doors?

Yes. Sliding doors, barn doors, and roll-up doors are all wrappable. Our installers cut and align the vinyl precisely at door seams so the design flows continuously across the door gap. We use edge-sealing techniques at door jambs to prevent lifting from repeated opening and closing.
