After the Wrap — Care, Warranty, and Reorders

Each Rivian R1T or R1S wrap comes with a robust 2-year workmanship warranty, meticulously covering any potential issues such as lifting, bubbling, edge peeling, and other installation defects. This warranty is distinct from the manufacturer’s vinyl warranty; both Avery Dennison and 3M offer their own film performance warranties, typically ranging from 5–7 years for outdoor durability.

To significantly extend the life and appearance of your Rivian wrap: always hand wash using a mild soap and water solution. Avoid automated brush car washes, as their abrasive action can scratch the vinyl and lift edges. When using pressure washers, maintain a distance of at least 12 inches from panel edges and keep the pressure below 1,200 PSI. Promptly address bird droppings and tree sap, as their acidic nature can etch into the vinyl if left for extended periods on your Rivian.

This ensures that reorders for new Rivian vehicles can enter production much faster, as the initial design approval process is already complete. Mastering the Rivian R1T & R1S: Unique Wrap Challenges & Solutions

Wrapping a Rivian R1T or R1S presents a distinct set of challenges compared to traditional internal combustion engine vehicles. The sleek, modern lines and integrated body panels demand an expert touch to ensure a flawless finish. Our technicians are meticulously trained to navigate the precise curves and unique contours of these electric adventure vehicles, guaranteeing a seamless application that preserves their cutting-edge aesthetic.

One of the primary considerations for Rivian wraps is the vehicle's expansive yet segmented surface area. The R1T, with its innovative Gear Tunnel and intricate bed design, requires careful paneling to avoid unsightly seams. Similarly, the R1S's continuous body lines, particularly around the D-pillar and rear quarter, necessitate precise material handling to achieve a paint-like finish. Our team meticulously plans every cut and stretch to conform perfectly to these advanced designs.

The Rivian's advanced sensor suite and charging ports also demand specialized attention during the wrapping process. We ensure that all critical components, from parking sensors to the charging door, remain fully functional and uncompromised. Our installation techniques are designed to integrate the wrap seamlessly around these features, maintaining the vehicle's factory precision and operational integrity, a crucial aspect for any high-tech EV.

Another key aspect is managing the vehicle's robust construction. The Rivian's durable exterior materials, while excellent for off-roading, require specific preparation techniques to ensure optimal adhesion of our premium cast vinyl. We employ specialized surface cleaning and adhesion promoters tailored for modern EV finishes, guaranteeing a long-lasting and bubble-free application that stands up to Chicago's diverse weather conditions.

Precision Panel Count & Surface Area for Rivian Wraps

The Rivian R1T and R1S, while similar in design language, feature distinct panel layouts that impact the wrapping process. The R1T, for instance, boasts a higher number of individual body panels due to its truck bed and innovative Gear Tunnel access. This requires more intricate cutting and precise alignment of wrap material, increasing the complexity and skill required for a flawless installation.

Conversely, the R1S, being an SUV, presents a more continuous body surface, particularly around the rear quarter panels and roofline. While seemingly simpler, these larger, uninterrupted sections demand exceptional material handling to prevent stretching or creasing. Our team expertly navigates these differences, understanding that each Rivian model requires a tailored approach to achieve impeccable results.

Understanding the precise surface area is crucial for accurate material estimation and labor allocation. A full wrap for a Rivian R1T typically involves covering approximately 200-220 square feet of exterior surface, accounting for its unique bed and Gear Tunnel. The R1S, with its enclosed rear, often falls in a similar range, around 190-210 square feet, depending on roof and bumper coverage.

The intricate panel count on both Rivian models, particularly around the unique lighting elements and integrated bumpers, contributes to the overall installation time. Our experienced technicians possess the nuanced skills to meticulously wrap these complex areas, ensuring every edge and curve is perfectly covered, delivering a finish that rivals a custom paint job.
