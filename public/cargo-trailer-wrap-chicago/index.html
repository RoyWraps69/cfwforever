How much does a cargo trailer wrap cost in Chicago?

Cargo trailer wraps in Chicago start at $2,800 for a 6x12 single-axle configuration. The popular 8.5x20 tandem runs $3,800–$4,500. 8.5x24 trailers run $4,500–$5,500. Is it worth wrapping a cargo trailer that isn't always moving?

Absolutely. A parked trailer is often more effective than a moving one. A wrapped 8.5x20 trailer parked in front of a remodel project in Bucktown or Wicker Park for 3 weeks generates thousands of impressions from residents, other contractors, and passersby. Your trailer becomes a job site billboard that follows you to every location.

Can you wrap both the sides and rear ramp of the trailer?

Yes — we wrap the full exterior: both sides, the rear door or ramp door, the V-nose or front panel, and optionally the roof. Rear door graphics are especially important since the trailer is viewed from behind in traffic. We plan rear door graphics to account for the door panel breaks and hardware.

Do trailer wraps hold up on trailers that are loaded and unloaded daily?

Yes — the side panels and front panel see minimal abuse from loading. The rear ramp door edge and lower side panels near the fenders are the most vulnerable areas. We recommend Avery Dennison MPI 1105 with overlaminate on these high-contact zones and standard cast vinyl on the upper panels.

After the Wrap — Care, Warranty, and Reorders

Every enclosed cargo trailer wrap comes with our robust 2-year workmanship warranty, which covers issues like lifting, bubbling, edge peeling, and any installation defects. This guarantee is separate from the manufacturer’s vinyl warranty; both Avery Dennison and 3M provide their own performance warranties of 5–7 years for outdoor use, ensuring the longevity of your trailer graphics.

To maximize the lifespan of your enclosed cargo trailer wrap, always hand wash it with mild soap and water, and avoid automated brush car washes as they can scratch and lift edges. When using pressure washers, keep them below 1,200 PSI and at least 12 inches from panel edges. Promptly address bird droppings and tree sap, as their acidic compounds can etch into the vinyl if left too long.

When your Chicago business expands its fleet and adds more enclosed cargo trailers, your original brand files are securely stored with us indefinitely. We never discard fleet artwork. This means reorders for new trailer wraps can go into production much faster, as the initial design approval is already complete.
