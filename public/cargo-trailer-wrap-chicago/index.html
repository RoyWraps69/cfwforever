What Makes Chicago Fleet Wraps Different for Cargo Trailer Installs

Aluminum trailer panels have exterior pop rivets that require vinyl relief cuts. The rear ramp door (on ramp-door trailers) needs vinyl termination at the hinge line with enough clearance for door operation. V-nose trailers have an angled front panel that requires 3D graphic planning to look correct from straight ahead.

We use only Avery Dennison MPI 1105 and 3M IJ180-CV3 cast vinyl — rated 5–7 years outdoor with proper care. Our HP Latex printers deliver Pantone-matched output on every job. Every installation carries a 2-year workmanship warranty covering lifting, bubbling, and edge peeling.

Frequently Asked Questions — Enclosed Cargo Trailer Wraps Chicago

How much does a cargo trailer wrap cost in Chicago?

Cargo trailer wraps in Chicago start at $2,800 for a 6x12 single-axle configuration. The popular 8.5x20 tandem runs $3,800–$4,500. 8.5x24 trailers run $4,500–$5,500. Trailer wraps generate exceptional value because the trailer parks at every job site — delivering location-specific impressions that a moving vehicle cannot.

Is it worth wrapping a cargo trailer that isn't always moving?

Absolutely. A parked trailer is often more effective than a moving one. A wrapped 8.5x20 trailer parked in front of a remodel project in Bucktown or Wicker Park for 3 weeks generates thousands of impressions from residents, other contractors, and passersby. Your trailer becomes a job site billboard that follows you to every location.

Can you wrap both the sides and rear ramp of the trailer?

Yes — we wrap the full exterior: both sides, the rear door or ramp door, the V-nose or front panel, and optionally the roof. Rear door graphics are especially important since the trailer is viewed from behind in traffic. We plan rear door graphics to account for the door panel breaks and hardware.

Do trailer wraps hold up on trailers that are loaded and unloaded daily?

Yes — the side panels and front panel see minimal abuse from loading. The rear ramp door edge and lower side panels near the fenders are the most vulnerable areas. We recommend Avery Dennison MPI 1105 with overlaminate on these high-contact zones and standard cast vinyl on the upper panels.
