Why Cast Vinyl is the Only Acceptable Commercial Wrap Material

In the Chicago commercial vehicle wrap sector, a significant issue with material quality persists. Many local wrap businesses offer services using calendered vinyl, an extruded film that is less expensive, typically priced at $4–$6 per square foot. This contrasts sharply with the $9.75 per square foot Chicago Fleet Wraps charges for superior cast vinyl. While the initial cost savings for fleet wraps might appear appealing, the long-term performance and aesthetic outcomes are often disappointing for businesses investing in vehicle graphics.

The production of calendered vinyl involves extruding liquid PVC under pressure through a die. This manufacturing method inherently creates internal molecular stresses within the film that cannot be entirely alleviated. These internal pressures, when subjected to Chicago's harsh environmental conditions—including its notorious freeze-thaw cycles, corrosive road salt, and intense UV radiation—cause the material to degrade prematurely, leading to cracking, peeling, and fading on fleet vehicles.

Conversely, the cast vinyl favored by Chicago Fleet Wraps, specifically Avery Dennison MPI 1105 and 3M IJ180-CV3, is produced by pouring liquid PVC onto a flat surface and then curing it. This distinct process effectively eliminates internal stresses, resulting in a dimensionally stable film that effortlessly conforms to the complex curves of various fleet vehicles. This robust material is engineered to endure Chicago's extreme temperatures and maintain its vibrant appearance and integrity for years, ensuring a lasting impression for your fleet.
