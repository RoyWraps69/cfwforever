Wrap Materials and Print Technology — What Goes on Your Fleet

Chicago Fleet Wraps exclusively uses 3M IJ180Cv3 and Avery Dennison MPI 1105 cast vinyl for commercial vehicle wraps. These are not the same materials used by budget shops running calendered vinyl. Cast vinyl is manufactured as a thin film (2 mil thickness) that conforms to compound curves, rivets, and corrugations without lifting or tenting. Calendered vinyl — the material used by shops charging $1,500 for a "full wrap" — is extruded under pressure and has memory, meaning it shrinks back toward its original shape over time, especially in Chicago's temperature extremes.

Our print process uses HP Latex 800W wide-format printers running at 1200 DPI resolution. Latex ink is water-based, VOC-free, and produces prints that resist UV fading for 5-7 years without lamination degradation. Every printed panel receives a 3M 8518 or Avery DOL 1460 over-laminate — a clear protective layer that shields the print from road debris, car wash brushes, salt spray, and UV exposure. This lamination layer is what separates a wrap that looks new after 5 years from one that fades and cracks after 18 months.

For commercial fleets running 10+ vehicles, we maintain material lot consistency across the entire order. This means every vehicle in your fleet has identical color matching — no variation between the first van wrapped and the tenth. We purchase vinyl in bulk rolls from the same production lot and store them in our climate-controlled 12,000 sq ft facility in Chicago to prevent any color shift from temperature or humidity exposure before installation.

Chicago Compliance — Permits, Regulations, and DOT Requirements

Commercial vehicle wraps in Chicago must comply with City of Chicago Municipal Code Chapter 9-80 regarding vehicle advertising. Wraps cannot obstruct the driver's view through any window, cannot contain obscene or misleading content, and must maintain the vehicle's DOT identification numbers visible if the vehicle is registered as a commercial motor vehicle over 10,001 lbs GVWR. Chicago Fleet Wraps handles all compliance considerations during the design phase — we template around DOT numbers, VIN plates, and required safety markings so your wrap is fully compliant from day one.

For box trucks and vehicles operating under FMCSA regulations, we ensure USDOT numbers and MC numbers remain visible and legible per 49 CFR 390.21. If your current DOT lettering needs to be updated or repositioned as part of the wrap, we include that in the scope of work at no additional charge. We also advise on Illinois Secretary of State requirements for commercial vehicle registration markings and can coordinate with your fleet manager to ensure every vehicle passes inspection after wrapping.

Insurance considerations are another area where experience matters. A properly installed commercial wrap does not affect your vehicle insurance premiums in Illinois. However, we recommend documenting the wrap investment with your insurance carrier so it is covered under your commercial auto policy in case of an accident. Chicago Fleet Wraps provides a detailed installation certificate with material specifications, warranty information, and replacement cost estimates that your insurance agent can add to your policy file.
