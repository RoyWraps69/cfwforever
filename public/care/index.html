Wrap Care & Maintenance Guide

How to make your Avery Dennison or 3M wrap last 7 years in Chicago. Proper care is the difference between a wrap that looks sharp at year 6 and one that fails at year 3 — especially in Chicago where road salt, winter chemicals, and freeze-thaw cycles accelerate wear.

🧼 Hand Wash Only💧 1,200 PSI Max Pressure❄️ Chicago Winter Tips🛡️ 2-Year Warranty

How to Make Your Wrap Last 7 Years

  • Wash method: Hand wash only. Soft cloth or sponge. Mild soap.
  • No automated brush car washes. Brush contact damages vinyl edges and lifts seams.
  • Pressure washers: Max 1,200 PSI. Nozzle at least 12" from surface. 40-degree spray angle. Never aim directly at panel edges.
  • Spot cleaning: Isopropyl alcohol (70%) for stubborn contamination. No solvents, acetone, or brake cleaner.
  • Wax: Carnauba-based wax only. No polymer sealants or ceramic coatings.
  • Parking: Garage storage when possible. UV and freeze-thaw cycling are the primary accelerants of fade and edge lift.

Chicago Winter Care

  • Road salt is the primary threat. Rinse with fresh water after each day of winter driving.
  • Never use de-icing chemicals directly on the wrap surface.
  • Use only soft plastic scrapers for ice, never metal.
  • If you notice edge lifting, contact the team immediately. Early re-sealing is a 15-minute repair.

What Voids the Warranty

  • Automated brush car washes
  • Pressure washing above 1,200 PSI or within 6" of the surface
  • Solvent-based cleaning products (WD-40, acetone, paint thinner)
  • Ceramic coating or paint protection film applied over vinyl
  • Impact damage, scrapes, or collision damage
