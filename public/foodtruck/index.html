Food Truck Design Process

Step 1: Consultation & Photography — We photograph your truck from every angle and take precise measurements of every panel, window, vent, and equipment mount. This creates an exact-scale digital template.

Step 2: Design Concepts — Our designers create 2–3 initial concepts incorporating your logo, brand colors, menu highlights, social media handles, and contact information. We show photorealistic mockups from every angle.

Step 3: Revisions & Approval — Unlimited revisions until you're 100% satisfied. We ensure the design works at speed (highway visibility), at rest (event lines), and on social media (Instagram-worthy).

Step 4: Print & Install — HP Latex 800W wide-format printing for vivid, food-safe colors. FDA-compliant overlaminate. Heat-resistant adhesive for kitchen-adjacent surfaces. 3–5 day installation.

FDA-Compliant Materials

All food truck wraps use materials specifically selected for food service environments:

  • FDA-Compliant Overlaminate — Food-safe protective layer that won't contaminate food contact areas.
  • Heat-Resistant Adhesive — Rated for surfaces near grills, fryers, and ovens up to 250°F.
  • Grease-Resistant Finish — Overlaminate protects against grease splatter, steam, and cooking oils.
  • Easy-Clean Surface — Smooth overlaminate allows daily cleaning without damaging the wrap.
  • UV-Resistant Ink — HP Latex inks resist fading from sun exposure at outdoor events.
  • Commercial-Grade Vinyl — Avery Dennison MPI 1105 or 3M IJ180-CV3 cast vinyl rated 5–7 years outdoor.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a food truck wrap cost in Chicago?

Full wraps range from $4,200–$8,000 depending on size and design complexity. Partial wraps start at $1,500. Menu boards start at $400. All pricing includes custom design, premium materials, and professional installation.

Are food truck wraps food-safe?

Yes. All wraps use FDA-compliant, food-safe overlaminate. Heat-resistant adhesive is used for surfaces near grills, fryers, and steam — ensuring the wrap stays intact and doesn't contaminate food contact areas.

How long does a food truck wrap last?

Premium cast vinyl is rated for 5–7 years outdoor. The overlaminate protects against grease, steam, and UV. Regular cleaning extends wrap life. Most food truck owners refresh their wraps every 3–5 years to update menus and branding.

Can you wrap around serving windows and equipment?

Yes. We template every food truck individually for serving windows, exhaust vents, generators, propane tanks, and equipment. Precision-cut for a seamless, professional finish.

Do you design food truck wraps?

Yes. Our design team specializes in food truck graphics. We create exact-scale templates, photorealistic mockups, and designs that work at highway speed, in event lines, and on social media.

How long does installation take?

Most food truck wraps take 3–5 business days. We accommodate rush timelines for festival season or grand openings — contact us for expedited service.

Can I update my menu boards without rewrapping the whole truck?

Yes. Menu board panels are designed as replaceable sections. Individual panel replacements start at $200–$400 and take 1 day.
