What Is the Downside of Wrapping a Car?

At Chicago Fleet Wraps, we love wrapping cars. But no product is perfect, and we believe in being honest. Here's the real deal on some potential downsides:

1. Not Permanent

Wraps last 5–7 years with premium cast vinyl (Avery Dennison MPI 1105 or 3M IJ180-CV3), but they're not forever. Calendered vinyl can degrade in as little as 1–2 years. Always ask your shop what material they use.

2. Quality Varies Wildly

A bad installation can bubble, peel, or lift within months. The difference between a $1,500 wrap and a $3,500 wrap is usually material quality and installer experience, not profit margin.

3. Surface Prep Matters

Wraps won't fix dents, rust, or peeling paint. The vehicle surface must be clean, smooth, and free of contaminants. Existing damage can show through or cause adhesion failure.

4. Washing Restrictions

Automated brush car washes and pressure washers above 1,200 PSI can damage wraps. Hand washing is recommended. For fleet operations, this means adjusting your wash routine.

The Bottom Line

Vehicle wraps are one of the highest-ROI marketing investments available — but only when done right. Choose a shop that uses cast vinyl exclusively, has verifiable experience, and offers a written warranty.
