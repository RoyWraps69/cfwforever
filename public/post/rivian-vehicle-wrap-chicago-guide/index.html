Why Rivian Requires a Different Installation Process

Rivian vehicles are manufactured with Thermal Plastic Olefin (TPO) exterior panels — a composite material used on the body side panels, fenders, and front and rear fascias of the R1T and R1S. TPO — a blend of thermoplastic rubber and polypropylene — has lower surface energy than painted steel panels, which affects vinyl adhesion differently than standard automotive substrates.

Standard cast vinyl adhesive systems are calibrated for painted steel and aluminum substrates with typical surface energy profiles of 35–42 dynes/cm. Rivian’s TPO panels measure 28–32 dynes/cm in unprimed condition. At this surface energy level, standard adhesive activation achieves approximately 70% of rated peel strength — sufficient for short-term adhesion but inadequate for the 5-year bond required on commercial fleet applications.

CFW’s EV-certified process for Rivian applies a two-part primer system on TPO panels: (1) a light isopropyl alcohol surface clean at 70% concentration with 90-second flash time, followed by (2) an Avery Dennison T-7000 adhesion promoter applied at 40µm wet film thickness and allowed to cure for 15 minutes before vinyl contact. This process achieves rated adhesion on TPO at CFW’s climate-controlled installation temperature of 70°F.

The Rivian EDV (Electric Delivery Van) is available in two configurations: the EDV 500 (500 cubic feet cargo volume) and the EDV 700 (700 cubic feet). CFW has installed fleet wraps on Amazon DSP Rivian EDV fleets across Illinois — maintaining consistent brand graphics across multi-unit fleet additions as operators scale their programs.

For non-Amazon commercial operators using Rivian EDVs in last-mile logistics, pest control, utilities, and telecommunications applications, CFW wraps EDV 700 units with operator-specific branding under standard fleet program terms. R1T and R1S Color Change Wraps

The Rivian R1T and R1S are popular color-change wrap candidates in the Chicago market. Rivian’s factory color palette is limited to six options as of the 2026 model year. CFW’s cast vinyl program offers 200+ color options including matte, satin, gloss, metallic, and color-shift finishes not available from Rivian at any price.

Color-change wraps on Rivian R1T and R1S vehicles require particular attention to the charge port surround — a panel with compound curves and a flush-fit door that must be wrapped to the perimeter edge with knifeless tape to maintain charge port function. CFW has developed a documented procedure for the R1T and R1S charge port surround wrap that allows complete coverage without impeding door operation.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to wrap a Rivian in Chicago?

A Rivian R1T pickup truck wrap costs $4,200–$5,200 at CFW. The Rivian R1S SUV runs $4,500–$5,400. The Rivian EDV 700 commercial delivery van costs $4,500–$5,000 for a full wrap. All pricing includes design, Avery Dennison cast vinyl, and EV-certified installation.

Can you wrap Rivian's composite body panels?

Yes. CFW is the only Illinois-certified installer applying HP Latex wraps to Rivian’s composite body panels as of 2026. The process requires specific surface preparation protocols for Rivian’s Thermal Plastic Olefin (TPO) exterior panels and modified adhesive activation temperatures. CFW has completed 600+ Rivian installations with zero paint damage claims.

Does wrapping a Rivian void the warranty?

No. Vehicle wraps do not void Rivian’s factory warranty. Under the Magnuson-Moss Warranty Act, Rivian cannot void a warranty because an aftermarket wrap was applied, unless Rivian proves the wrap caused the specific damage claimed. Professional cast vinyl installation and removal causes no damage to Rivian’s composite panels.

What vinyl works best on Rivian vehicles?

CFW uses Avery Dennison MPI 1105 cast vinyl on all Rivian installations. Rivian’s TPO composite panels require a low-energy-surface primer in some applications — particularly on the EDV 700 cargo door and front fascia. CFW assesses primer requirements during pre-installation inspection at no additional charge.

How long does a Rivian EDV 700 fleet wrap take to install?

A Rivian EDV 700 full wrap installation takes 2–2.5 days at CFW’s facility in Portage Park, Chicago. The additional time vs. a standard cargo van reflects the EDV 700’s composite panel geometry and charge port surround detail work. Fleet programs of 10+ EDV units operate on a rolling installation schedule.
