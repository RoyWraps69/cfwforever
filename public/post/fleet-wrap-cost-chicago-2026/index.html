The Real Cost After IRS Section 179 Deduction

Vehicle wraps are classified as advertising expenses under IRS Publication 535 and qualify for full deduction in the year of installation under Section 179. This distinction — advertising vs. capital improvement — is why wraps are deductible and paint jobs are not. Paint is a vehicle improvement; wraps are advertising.

Fleet Wrap Cost vs. Fleet Vehicle Paint — Chicago 2026

A professional paint job on a cargo van in the Chicago metro costs $3,000–$6,000 installed, takes 5–10 business days, cannot be reversed, and is classified as a vehicle improvement — not deductible as advertising. A full vinyl wrap installs in 1–2 days, is fully reversible, is 100% tax deductible, and protects the factory paint underneath.
