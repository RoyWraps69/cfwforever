Frequently Asked Questions — Step Van / Walk-In Van Wraps Chicago

How much does a step van wrap cost in Chicago?

Step van wraps in Chicago start at $5,500 for standard Workhorse and Grumman configurations. Larger 20–22 ft walk-in vans run $6,500–$7,500. The vertical, flat side panels of step vans are among the best graphic canvases in commercial vehicles — the price-to-impression ratio is exceptional.

Can you wrap a food service step van?

Yes — food service step vans are one of our specialty categories. The large flat panels are perfect for food photography, menu items, and bold brand colors. We have wrapped step vans for Chicago bakeries, catering companies, and specialty food distributors. The rear step and side service windows require special vinyl planning to maintain functionality.

How do you handle the panel seams on older step vans?

Older Workhorse and Grumman step vans have visible exterior panel seams. We plan graphic designs to align natural visual breaks with these seams, then bridge the vinyl across the seam with careful overlap. On older vans with oxidized aluminum panels, surface cleaning and prep is required before installation.

Do step van wraps hold up well on delivery vehicles?

Yes — step van wraps hold up well on the vertical side panels which see minimal contact. The vulnerable areas are the lower panels below the step and the rear door edges which can be scratched by cargo. We recommend our standard Avery Dennison MPI 1105 with DOL 1360 overlaminate for maximum durability on working step vans.

After the Wrap — Care, Warranty, and Reorders

Each step van wrap is backed by a 2-year workmanship warranty, covering issues such as lifting, bubbling, edge peeling, and any installation defects. This warranty is distinct from the manufacturer's vinyl warranty; both Avery Dennison and 3M provide their own film performance warranties, typically spanning 5–7 years for outdoor usage on commercial vehicles.

To maximize the longevity of your step van wrap, always hand wash it with mild soap and water. Avoid automated brush car washes, which can scratch the graphics and lift edges on these large vehicles. When using pressure washers, keep the PSI below 1,200 and maintain a distance of at least 12 inches from panel edges. Promptly address bird droppings and tree sap, as their acidic compounds can etch into the vinyl if left for extended periods.

When you expand your fleet with additional step vans months or even years down the line, your specific brand files for these commercial vehicles are permanently archived with us. We never discard fleet artwork. This ensures reorders for new step van wraps go into production much faster, as the design approval process is already complete. Optimizing Your Step Van Wrap's Lifespan in the Windy City

To ensure your step van wrap maintains its vibrant appearance and advertising efficacy across Chicago's varying conditions, proper maintenance is key. We recommend regular, gentle hand washing with mild soap and water, avoiding abrasive brushes or high-pressure washers that can compromise the vinyl's integrity. Focus on removing road grime, salt residue (especially after winter driving), and environmental pollutants that can accumulate during routes through areas like the industrial South Side or heavy traffic zones.

While our premium Avery Dennison MPI 1105 and 3M IJ180-CV3 cast vinyls are engineered for durability, protecting your investment with routine care will significantly extend its lifespan. Parking your step van in shaded areas or garages when possible can also mitigate prolonged UV exposure, which can slowly degrade even the best materials over many years.
