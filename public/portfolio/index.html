9,400+ Commercial Wraps Across Every Vehicle Type

The portfolio you're looking at represents 24 years of commercial vehicle wrap work across Chicago and the surrounding Chicagoland area. Every image shows a real job, installed in our shop on Lamon Ave, photographed after completion. No stock images, no out-of-market work, no renders. What you see is what we do.

We started in Las Vegas in 2001 and relocated to Chicago in 2014. In Las Vegas, we worked heavily in hospitality, entertainment, and the gaming industry. In Chicago, our client base shifted toward commercial service fleets — HVAC operators, electrical contractors, plumbing companies, landscapers, delivery networks, and moving companies. The Chicago market rewarded specialization in commercial fleet work, and that's where we focused.

Today, the majority of our work is fleet accounts — companies with 3 to 300+ vehicles that rely on us as their long-term wrap partner. We've wrapped Improovy Painters' cargo van fleet across multiple rounds of vehicle additions. We've done Arnold Electric's full transit van fleet. We've handled MH Equipment's regional van program. We've wrapped every Rivian that Blue Origin sent through our doors, and we're the shop Amazon DSP operators in the Midwest call when they need high-volume Rivian R1V wraps done right.

What the Portfolio Covers

Cargo vans are the backbone of our commercial work. Ford Transit Connect, Ford E-Series, Chevy Express, GMC Savana — we've wrapped thousands of them. The Improovy Painters series in the portfolio shows what a well-designed cargo van wrap looks like at scale: consistent across every vehicle in the fleet, production-efficient to install, and built to last the 5-7 year lifespan of the van itself.

Full-size transit vans — Ford Transit high-roof, Mercedes Sprinter, Ram ProMaster — represent our fastest-growing category. HVAC contractors, medical transport operators, and specialty service companies have shifted heavily toward Sprinter-type vehicles in the last decade, and the wrap work follows. The Medxwaste transit series in the portfolio is a good example of a full-bleed commercial fleet wrap that works at highway speed and in tight parking situations.

Box trucks are a different challenge. The large flat panels create opportunities for bold graphic design, but they also amplify any installation imperfection. Our box truck portfolio — Oakbros, Windy City Movers, Roza Contractors — shows work on vehicles ranging from 14-foot cube vans to 26-foot straight trucks. Every panel seam is planned, every graphic element is scaled and positioned for real-world viewing distances.

The Rivian section of the portfolio reflects our specialized expertise in EV fleet wraps. We've wrapped more than 600 Rivian R1T pickups and R1S SUVs — more than any other shop in Illinois. The Blue Origin Rivian series, the Amazon Prime delivery van program, and the multi-color custom R1S builds in the portfolio show the range of what's possible on the Rivian platform.

Color Change and Specialty Finishes

Not all of our work is commercial fleet. Color change wraps — matte black, satin finishes, holographic films, carbon fiber vinyl, chrome delete — represent a significant portion of our passenger vehicle work. The Tesla Cybertruck series in the portfolio, the holographic car wrap, and the chrome-wrapped vehicles show the aesthetic range on the specialty finish side.

Chicago's automotive culture runs deep. We work with car enthusiasts throughout the city — Lincoln Park, Wicker Park, Gold Coast, the western suburbs — who want a finish that factory paint can't deliver. Every color change wrap uses cast vinyl only. We don't cut corners on material selection regardless of whether the vehicle is a fleet van or a personal car.

Chicago and Chicagoland Coverage

Every job in this portfolio came through our shop on Lamon Ave in Chicago's Portage Park neighborhood. We don't do mobile installs — commercial vehicle wraps require a climate-controlled, dust-controlled environment to install correctly. Every vehicle in this portfolio was installed in a heated, temperature-controlled bay where the vinyl could be applied at the right temperature and humidity for a proper bond.

Our clients come from across the Chicago metro — from Andersonville and Rogers Park on the North Side to Hyde Park and Bronzeville on the South Side, from the Loop to the western suburbs of Naperville, Schaumburg, and Elmhurst. We offer free fleet pickup for accounts with 3 or more vehicles. Our driver will pick up your vans, deliver them to the shop, and return them when the work is complete.
