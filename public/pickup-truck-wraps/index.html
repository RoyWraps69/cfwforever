Pickup Truck Wrap Specs — F-150, Silverado, RAM

Material by model: Ford F-150 crew cab full wrap: 230–260 sq ft. Chevy Silverado 1500: 240–270 sq ft. RAM 1500: 235–265 sq ft. The bed adds 60–80 sq ft depending on bed length (5.5 ft vs 6.5 ft).

Truck-specific challenges: Pickup beds take the most abuse — tools, cargo, and loading dock contact. We recommend skipping the inner bed walls and focusing on the exterior panels, tailgate, and bed caps. For work trucks, we apply a clear PPF strip along the bed rail to prevent wrap damage from loading.

Pricing: Full wrap (cab + bed exterior): $3,200–$4,500. Cab-only wrap: $2,400–$3,400. Tailgate wrap only: $400–$800. The tailgate-only option is surprisingly effective for contractors — it's the most visible panel when the truck is parked at a job site.

Full vs. Partial Pickup Truck Wraps: Tailoring Your Visual Impact

Deciding between a full and partial wrap for your F-150, Ram 1500, or Silverado depends on your marketing goals and budget. A full wrap provides maximum visual impact, covering the entire painted surface of your truck with your custom design. This option offers comprehensive branding, turning your vehicle into a smooth, eye-catching advertisement that commands attention from Evanston to Orland Park.

Partial wraps, on the other hand, strategically cover significant portions of your pickup, often using the truck's original paint color as part of the design. This can be a highly effective and more budget-friendly option, focusing on key areas like the doors, tailgate, or bed sides. For instance, a partial wrap on a Ford Ranger might highlight your logo and contact information prominently, providing excellent brand recognition without the extensive coverage of a full wrap. Full wraps typically range from $3,500-$6,500, while partial wraps are often $1,800-$3,800 depending on coverage.
