The Selection Process

HOW TO CHOOSE A CAR WRAP SHOP IN CHICAGO

Chicago has dozens of wrap shops. Most are fine. A few are great. Here's how to tell the difference — and what separates shops that do 50 wraps a year from shops like us that do 9,400+.

WHAT TO ASK EVERY SHOP

What vinyl do you use?

The answer should be Avery Dennison MPI 1105 or 3M IJ180-CV3. If they say "premium vinyl" without naming the manufacturer, they're using budget material. If they mention any brand you don't recognize, that's calendered vinyl and it will fail within 2 years.

Is your install bay climate controlled?

In Chicago, this matters. Vinyl applied in cold or hot conditions doesn't adhere correctly. The adhesive needs 68–75°F to activate properly. Any shop working in an unheated garage in winter is producing inferior installs.

Do you offer a warranty?

A legitimate installer warrants their workmanship. Our standard is 2 years. Lift edges, peeling, or application failures within that window are fixed at no charge. A shop that won't warranty work doesn't believe in it.

Can I see recent installs in person?

Portfolio photos are easy to curate. Asking to see a vehicle they wrapped 6 months ago tells you everything about how the install holds up over time. We welcome this request.

RED FLAGS TO AVOID

✗
Price too low to be cast vinyl — Full wrap under $1,500 for a sedan means calendered vinyl. Material cost alone for a proper install is $600–$900.
✗
1-day turnaround on a full wrap — A properly done full color change takes 2–4 days minimum. 1-day means they're cutting corners on prep, application, or both.
✗
No physical location — Mobile wrap "services" that install in parking lots or driveways cannot control temperature, dust, or contamination. Quality installs require a controlled environment.
✗
No warranty offered — Self-explanatory. A shop that won't stand behind their work is telling you what they think of it.
✗
Visible seams on primary panels — Check portfolio photos for seam lines running across hoods, roofs, or doors. Good installs hide seams in body lines and gaps. Visible center-of-panel seams are a material or skill issue.
After the Wrap

KEEPING YOUR CAR WRAP SHARP IN CHICAGO

Chicago winters are hard on vehicles. Salt, freeze-thaw cycling, and UV exposure in summer are the three main threats to your wrap's lifespan. Here's the maintenance protocol we give every customer:

✓
RINSE WEEKLY IN WINTER

Road salt accelerates edge lift. A quick rinse — even touchless — removes salt before it works under edges.

✓
HAND WASH ONLY

Brush car washes abrade the surface over time. Touchless is acceptable; brushes are not. Use a vinyl-safe soap.

✓
NO WAX ON MATTE OR SATIN

Wax creates glossy spots on flat finishes. Use a spray vinyl sealant designed for matte surfaces instead.

✓
ADDRESS LIFT EDGES IMMEDIATELY

If an edge starts to lift, it's a 5-minute fix — or a peeled panel in two weeks. Call us. We handle warranty work fast.

✓
GARAGE STORAGE ADDS YEARS

UV exposure and freeze-thaw are the primary life reducers. Indoor parking can add 1–2 years to a wrap's life in Chicago's climate.

VEHICLE-SPECIFIC NOTES

TESLA & EV OWNERS

EV charging cables and charging port covers need extra attention during washes. We wrap around charging ports — not over them. Routine is the same: hand wash, no brush, rinse salt in winter.

TRUCKS & SUVS

Lower rocker panels and wheel wells accumulate salt and road debris faster. Pay extra attention to these areas during winter rinses. Check rocker seams monthly for early lift.

LEASED VEHICLES

Schedule removal before lease turn-in. We remove wraps as a service and the factory paint will be in better shape than if it had been exposed. Book at least 2 weeks before turn-in date.
