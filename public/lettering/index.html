Cut Vinyl vs. Printed Graphics

Cut vinyl lettering uses solid-color vinyl cut into individual letters and shapes. It's clean, professional, and extremely durable — lasting 7–10 years outdoors. For simple text and single-color logos, cut vinyl is the most cost-effective and longest-lasting option.

Printed graphics allow full-color logos, photos, and complex designs. We print on premium Avery Dennison cast vinyl with UV overlaminate for 5–7 years of outdoor durability. Best for multi-color logos and photographic elements.

DOT Number Requirements

The Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA) requires USDOT numbers on both sides of all commercial vehicles that meet any of these criteria:

  • Gross Vehicle Weight Rating (GVWR) over 10,001 lbs
  • Transporting hazardous materials requiring placards
  • Operating in interstate commerce for compensation

Requirements: letters must be at least 2 inches tall, in a color that contrasts with the vehicle's background, and visible from 50 feet. We ensure full FMCSA compliance on every DOT lettering job.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does vehicle lettering cost?

DOT numbers start at $150. Basic lettering (name + phone) starts at $250. Full packages with logo, name, phone, and website start at $500.

What are DOT number requirements?

USDOT numbers are required on both sides of commercial vehicles over 10,001 lbs GVW. Letters must be 2"+ tall in a contrasting color. We ensure full FMCSA compliance.

How long does vinyl lettering last?

Cut vinyl lettering lasts 7–10 years outdoors. Printed graphics last 5–7 years with UV overlaminate.

Cut vinyl vs. printed — which should I choose?

Cut vinyl for simple text and single-color logos. Printed graphics for full-color logos and complex designs.

Can you letter an entire fleet?

Yes. Fleet programs with volume pricing, consistent templates, and free pickup. We store your templates for future additions.
