Selection Criteria

What Separates the Best Vehicle Wrap Shops

✓ Cast Vinyl Only

Cast vinyl conforms to vehicle contours and holds color for 5–7 years outdoors. Calendered vinyl shrinks, cracks, and fails on commercial vehicles within 2–3 years — especially in Chicago winters. Any serious wrap shop specifies cast vinyl as standard. Ask for the product name. Avery MPI 1105 and 3M IJ180-CV3 are the industry standards. If they can't name the film, walk away.

✓ Climate-Controlled Install Bay

Vinyl applied below 60°F or above 90°F bonds poorly and fails early. A professional shop installs in a heated, temperature-controlled environment year-round — critical in Chicago, where temperatures swing from -20°F to 100°F. Ask if they wrap outdoors or in unheated bays in winter. If yes, that's a red flag.

✓ 3M or Avery Dennison Certification

Both manufacturers maintain online directories of certified installers. Certified shops complete factory training, use approved materials, and access extended material warranties. Check the Avery Dennison installer directory and the 3M certified installer list. Chicago Fleet Wraps appears in both.

✓ Commercial Fleet Portfolio

Fleet wraps on cargo vans, Sprinters, and box trucks require different techniques than single passenger cars. Look for a verifiable portfolio of commercial fleet work — not just car wraps. Ask for client references. A shop that has wrapped 50 cargo vans for a single fleet client knows what production consistency looks like. One that wraps 2 cars a week does not.

✓ Warranty in Writing

A minimum 2-year workmanship warranty on labor and materials is standard for any reputable shop. Get it in writing. Ask what the warranty covers — edge lifting, fading, peeling. Ask what voids it. Chicago Fleet Wraps provides a 2-year workmanship warranty plus the manufacturer's 5-year material warranty on all fleet work.

✓ In-House Design and Print

Shops that design, print, and install under one roof maintain quality control through every step. When design and print are outsourced, the install team often has no input on color calibration, bleed zones, or panel seam placement. FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I look for in a vehicle wrap shop in Chicago?

3M or Avery Dennison certification, a climate-controlled install bay, a verifiable commercial fleet portfolio, and a written warranty of at least 2 years on materials and labor. Always ask which vinyl film they use by name — the answer tells you everything.

How do I verify a wrap shop's certifications?

Both 3M and Avery Dennison maintain online directories of certified installers. Check them directly. Ask the shop for their certification documentation. Chicago Fleet Wraps is listed in both directories and can provide documentation on request.

How much does a commercial vehicle wrap cost in Chicago?

How long does a vehicle wrap last in Chicago weather?

5–7 years for a properly installed cast vinyl wrap. Chicago's freeze-thaw cycles and road salt are hard on wraps installed with calendered vinyl or applied at incorrect temperatures. Cast vinyl from certified manufacturers holds up. Chicago Fleet Wraps warrants all wraps for 2 years on workmanship and 5 years on materials.

What is the difference between a certified and non-certified wrap shop?

Certified shops complete factory training, use manufacturer-approved materials, and access factory warranty support. Non-certified shops have no manufacturer backing. For fleet work where dozens of vehicles need to match, certification ensures consistency.
