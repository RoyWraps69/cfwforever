What Separates the Best Wrap Shops

Cast Vinyl Only

Cast vinyl conforms to vehicle contours and holds color for 5-7 years. Calendered vinyl shrinks, cracks, and fails on commercial vehicles within 2-3 years. Any reputable shop specifies cast vinyl as standard.

Climate-Controlled Bay

Vinyl applied below 60°F or above 90°F bonds poorly and fails early. A professional shop installs in a heated, temperature-controlled environment year-round — critical in Chicago winters.

Manufacturer Certification

3M and Avery Dennison certify installers who complete factory training. Certified shops access extended material warranties. Check the 3M and Avery online directories before booking.

Commercial Fleet Experience

Fleet wraps on cargo vans, Sprinters, and box trucks require different techniques than single passenger cars. Look for a verifiable portfolio of commercial fleet work — not just car wraps.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I look for in a vehicle wrap shop in Chicago?

Look for 3M or Avery Dennison certification, a climate-controlled install bay, verifiable portfolio of commercial fleet work, and a warranty of at least 2 years on materials and labor. Avoid shops that use calendered vinyl.

How long does a vehicle wrap last?

A properly installed cast vinyl wrap lasts 5-7 years. Chicago Fleet Wraps uses only cast vinyl installed in a climate-controlled facility.

What is the difference between certified and non-certified wrap shops?

Certified shops complete factory training, use approved materials, and can access manufacturer warranty support. Non-certified shops have no manufacturer backing.
