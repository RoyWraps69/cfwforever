Why Wrap Instead of Paint?

Marine paint jobs cost 2–3x more than a wrap, take weeks to cure, and are permanent. A vinyl wrap can be removed without damaging the gelcoat, installed in 3–5 days, and changed whenever you want a new look. Wraps also protect the underlying gelcoat from UV damage, scratches, and oxidation — preserving your boat's resale value.

For commercial operators — charter companies, tour boats, fishing charters — a wrapped boat is a floating billboard that attracts customers at the marina, on the water, and in every photo your passengers post on social media.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a boat wrap cost?

Small boats (under 20 ft) cost $2,500–$5,000. Mid-size boats (20–30 ft) cost $5,000–$10,000. Large boats and yachts (30+ ft) start at $10,000+. Jet ski wraps cost $800–$1,500. All pricing depends on boat size and design complexity.

Will a boat wrap hold up in Lake Michigan?

Yes. We use marine-grade cast vinyl with UV-resistant and saltwater-resistant overlaminate specifically designed for marine environments. With proper installation and maintenance, marine wraps last 3–5 years in water exposure conditions.

Can you wrap a boat hull?

Yes. We wrap above-waterline hulls, transoms, cabins, and deck surfaces. Below-waterline wraps require specialized antifouling vinyl — contact us for details on full-hull applications.

How long does boat wrap installation take?

Most boat wraps take 3–5 business days depending on size and complexity. The boat needs to be clean, dry, and out of the water for installation. We can coordinate with your marina or storage facility.
