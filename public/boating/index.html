Why Wrap Instead of Paint?

Marine paint jobs are expensive, permanent, and time-consuming. A quality marine paint job costs $15,000–$30,000+ for a mid-size boat, takes weeks to cure, and can't be changed without sanding and repainting. A vinyl wrap achieves the same visual result for 40–60% less cost, installs in 3–5 days, and can be removed or changed whenever you want a new look.

Wraps also protect the underlying gelcoat from UV damage, scratches, minor impacts, and oxidation. When the wrap is removed — whether for a new design or before selling — the gelcoat underneath is in better condition than unprotected surfaces. This preserves and often increases your boat's resale value.

For commercial operators — charter companies, tour boats, fishing charters, water taxis — a wrapped boat is a floating billboard. Every passenger who boards, every spectator on the shore, and every photo posted on social media amplifies your brand reach. Wraps can be updated seasonally or for special events without the cost of repainting.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a boat wrap cost in Chicago?

Jet ski wraps cost $800–$1,500. Small boats (under 20 ft) cost $2,500–$5,000. Mid-size boats (20–30 ft) cost $5,000–$10,000. Large boats and yachts (30+ ft) start at $10,000+. Transom lettering starts at $300.

Will a boat wrap hold up in Lake Michigan?

Yes. Marine-grade cast vinyl with UV-resistant and saltwater-resistant overlaminate is designed for marine environments. With proper installation and maintenance, wraps last 3–5 years in water exposure conditions.

Can you wrap a boat hull?

Yes. We wrap above-waterline hulls, transoms, cabins, and deck surfaces. Below-waterline wraps use specialized antifouling vinyl — contact us for details.

How long does boat wrap installation take?

Most boat wraps take 3–5 business days. The boat must be clean, dry, and out of the water. We coordinate with your marina or storage facility for seamless scheduling.

Is a boat wrap cheaper than marine paint?

Yes — typically 40–60% less expensive. Marine paint costs $15,000–$30,000+ for mid-size boats. A wrap achieves the same result for $5,000–$10,000 and can be removed without damaging gelcoat.

Can you wrap a pontoon boat?

Yes. We wrap pontoon fencing panels, consoles, and accent areas. Pontoon wraps are popular for both personal customization and commercial charter use on Chicago waterways.

Do boat wraps protect the gelcoat?

Yes. Vinyl wraps protect against UV damage, scratches, minor impacts, and oxidation. When removed, the gelcoat underneath is in better condition — preserving resale value.
