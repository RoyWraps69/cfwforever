Why Cast Vinyl is the Only Acceptable Commercial Wrap Material

Within the commercial vehicle wrap sector, a persistent issue revolves around material quality. Numerous Chicago-based wrap providers offer services utilizing calendered vinyl, an extruded film that is less expensive, typically priced at $4–6 per square foot. In contrast, Chicago Fleet Wraps employs cast vinyl, which costs $9.75 per square foot, a price point reflecting its superior quality. While the initial cost savings of calendered options may seem appealing for a project like the Blue Origin Rivian EDV fleet, the long-term performance and aesthetic outcomes are significantly compromised.

The manufacturing process for calendered vinyl involves extruding liquid PVC through a die under high pressure. This method introduces internal molecular stresses within the film that are inherently difficult to alleviate completely. Over time, these residual stresses lead to material degradation. This degradation is further exacerbated by Chicago's harsh environmental conditions, including its characteristic freeze-thaw cycles, corrosive road salt, and intense UV radiation, all of which would prematurely compromise the integrity and appearance of a Blue Origin Rivian EDV fleet wrap.

For the demanding application of a Blue Origin Rivian EDV fleet wrap, cast vinyl is the superior choice, specifically grades like Avery Dennison MPI 1105 and 3M IJ180-CV3. This material is produced by casting liquid PVC onto a flat surface, allowing it to cure without introducing internal stress. This results in a dimensionally stable film that effortlessly conforms to the complex contours of the Rivian EDV, endures Chicago's extreme temperatures, and maintains its vibrant imagery and structural integrity over an extended lifespan, crucial for a high-profile brand like Blue Origin.
