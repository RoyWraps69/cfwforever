Why Cast Vinyl is the Only Acceptable Commercial Wrap Material

The commercial vehicle wrap industry faces a significant issue regarding material quality. Many Chicago-based walk-in shops offer wraps using calendered vinyl, an extruded film that's less expensive, priced at roughly $4–6 per square foot. In contrast, Chicago Fleet Wraps utilizes cast vinyl for Amazon DSP vehicles, which costs $9.75 per square foot. While the initial cost savings of calendered vinyl may seem appealing for your Amazon DSP fleet in Chicago, the long-term performance and appearance are undeniably inferior.

Calendered vinyl is produced by forcing liquid PVC through a die under high pressure. This manufacturing method introduces internal molecular stresses within the film that are impossible to completely eliminate. Over time, these inherent stresses, particularly when exposed to Chicago's harsh freeze-thaw cycles, corrosive road salt, and intense UV radiation, inevitably lead to the material's premature degradation, making it an unsuitable choice for the rigorous demands of Amazon DSP delivery vehicles.

For your Amazon DSP fleet in Chicago, we exclusively use cast vinyl, specifically Avery Dennison MPI 1105 and 3M IJ180-CV3. This premium material is crafted by pouring liquid PVC onto a flat surface and then curing it. This superior process eliminates internal stresses, resulting in a dimensionally stable film that perfectly conforms to the complex curves of Rivian EDVs, withstands Chicago's extreme temperatures, and maintains its vibrant appearance throughout its lifespan.
