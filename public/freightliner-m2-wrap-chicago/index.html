After the Wrap — Care, Warranty, and Reorders

Every Freightliner M2 106 wrap installation is backed by a 2-year workmanship warranty, covering potential issues such as lifting, bubbling, edge peeling, and any installation defects. This is distinct from the vinyl manufacturer's warranty; both Avery Dennison and 3M provide their own robust film performance warranties, typically ranging from 5 to 7 years for outdoor durability.

To maximize the lifespan and appearance of your Freightliner M2 106 wrap, we recommend hand washing with mild soap and water. Avoid automated brush car washes, as they can scratch the vinyl and lift edges. When using pressure washers, keep the PSI below 1,200 and maintain a distance of at least 12 inches from panel edges. Promptly address bird droppings and tree sap; these acidic compounds can etch into the vinyl if left for extended periods.

Mastering the Scale: Freightliner M2 106 Wrap Specifications & Challenges

Wrapping a substantial vehicle like the Freightliner M2 106 presents a unique set of challenges and opportunities, demanding precision and specialized expertise. This robust box truck, often seen traversing major Chicago arteries like I-55 or Lake Shore Drive, boasts an expansive surface area, typically ranging from 450 to 650 square feet depending on the box length and cab configuration. Our meticulous approach ensures every inch, from the aerodynamic cab to the extensive cargo box, receives flawless application of our premium Avery Dennison MPI 1105 or 3M IJ180-CV3 cast vinyl.

The sheer size of the M2 106 necessitates a multi-panel installation strategy, often involving 12-18 distinct vinyl sections for a full wrap. This complexity requires our certified technicians to precisely align graphics across large, flat surfaces, while also expertly navigating the various rivets, hinges, and intricate body lines inherent to commercial truck design. Unlike smaller vehicles, the M2 106 often features utility boxes, side skirts, and fuel tanks that require careful consideration or strategic exclusion from the wrap design to ensure longevity and a clean finish.

Specific challenges we routinely overcome with the Freightliner M2 106 include managing the articulation points between the cab and the box, ensuring proper vinyl adhesion around rear door hardware, and seamlessly integrating graphics across the ribbed or corrugated panels often found on box truck bodies. Our team's extensive experience with over 9,400 vehicles, including numerous M2 106 models for businesses from Lincoln Park to the far South Side, means we anticipate these nuances, guaranteeing a bubble-free, perfectly aligned wrap that stands up to Chicago's demanding climate.
